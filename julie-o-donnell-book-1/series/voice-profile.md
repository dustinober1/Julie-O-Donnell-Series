# Voice Profile

## Author intent

- Deliver a compounding sense of systemic paranoia and claustrophobic tension.
- Make invisible digital systems feel weaponized through consequence rather than lecture.
- Leave the reader with dread that automated escalation can outrun human correction.

## Positive voice evidence

- The writer sample repeatedly places concrete physical evidence beside abstract institutional risk so the physical world can test the digital claim.
- The narration stays close to the focal character’s inspection habits, privileging what can be verified, measured, heard, timed, or physically felt.
- Tension builds by narrowing time, multiplying procedural obstacles, and letting official language carry threat.
- Setting detail is specific and functional rather than decorative; the environment often acts as a reality check against system certainty.

## Sentence and paragraph behavior

- Favor short-to-medium sentences under pressure, with longer sentences used to stack procedural consequence or controlled observation.
- Use brief standalone lines to land recognition, contradiction, or irreversible status changes.
- Keep paragraphs compact, especially once a scene shifts from observation to escalation.
- Let technical phrasing appear in clean bursts that sharpen pressure instead of pausing the scene for tutorial explanation.

## Dialogue behavior

- Dialogue is concise, adversarial, and information-bearing.
- Characters reveal hierarchy, trust, and institutional self-protection through what they omit, deflect, or finally admit.
- Avoid ornamental banter; exchanges should alter risk, authority, or available options.
- Preserve dry wit only when it tightens pressure rather than releasing it.

## Emotional restraint and intensity

- Stay in close third with a defensive, clinical filter.
- Let dread emerge from narrowed options, physiological response, and observational fixation rather than explicit emotional explanation.
- Reserve direct naming of emotion for rare moments when suppression fails.
- Intensify scenes by increasing precision rather than inflating rhetoric.

## Productive imperfections to preserve

- Keep procedural density when systems, routing, custody, or bureaucracy become the threat surface.
- Preserve clipped fragments and abrupt paragraph turns at peaks of vigilance.
- Allow controlled repetition when the character is testing signal, time, or causality under stress.
- Do not over-smooth the prose into easy reassurance.

## Not-this-author evidence

- Avoid long explanatory lectures about systems, code, or policy that halt momentum.
- Avoid generic action-hero swagger, invulnerable competence theater, or spectacle-first violence.
- Avoid turning institutional critique into speechmaking.
- Avoid glamorized digital intrusion or theatrical rebelliousness.

## Permissioned lived material

- The work may portray the procedural and ethical pressure around military intelligence and automated targeting, but it must not claim intimate ownership of active-duty combat trauma beyond what the evidence supports.
- It may depict institutional language and decision pressure, but it must not invent insider certainty about the private upper-echelon culture of intelligence organizations.
- It may use the border-region setting as a source of geopolitical consequence, but it must not counterfeit localized lived experience as firsthand truth.
- When the draft reaches beyond verified knowledge, prefer bounded observation, sourced procedure, or explicit distance.
