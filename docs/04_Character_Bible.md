# 04 — Character Bible

## Character status labels

- **Locked:** use as canon unless deliberately revised.
- **Provisional:** usable for planning but should be confirmed before final drafting.
- **Placeholder:** structural role only; name/details may change.

## Julie O'Donnell

**Status:** Locked core protagonist, provisional details.

### Identity

- Former U.S. Army Captain.
- Former Army intelligence officer.
- Present-day civilian living in or near Culpeper, Virginia.
- Associated with a farmhouse, horses, fence work, and practical outdoor routines.

### Core wound

Julie saw an AI-enabled intelligence/targeting system produce a confidence pattern that was too clean to trust. She warned command that the data was engineered. The system and command chain moved anyway. The event ended or destroyed her Army career and taught her that being right does not protect you when the institution needs certainty.

### Core competence

Julie is not a hacker stereotype. She is an intelligence professional with exceptional pattern recognition and operational skepticism. She understands:

- SIGINT workflows;
- targeting pressure;
- confidence scores;
- how bad data can be made to look clean;
- how analysts behave around command expectations;
- how institutions punish inconvenient uncertainty.

### External presentation

Julie should read as controlled, dry, tired of euphemisms, and allergic to bullshit. She does not need to be constantly sarcastic, but she should have a low tolerance for polished language that hides responsibility.

### Emotional profile

- Functional trauma, not melodrama.
- Hyper-aware of pressure patterns.
- Distrusts systems that remove human accountability.
- Suspicious when confidence arrives too quickly.
- Does not want to be needed by the people who broke her.

### Strengths

- Sees manufactured patterns.
- Keeps thinking under pressure.
- Resists rank intimidation when the data is wrong.
- Understands both technical systems and human command behavior.
- Can explain complex danger in blunt human terms.

### Weaknesses

- Can isolate.
- Can assume institutions are acting in bad faith even when individual people are not.
- May resist help until too late.
- May underplay her own emotional damage.
- May be baited by people who frame her as unstable or bitter.

### Character arc for Book 1

From: "I survived by leaving the system."

To: "Leaving the system did not stop it from growing; I have to choose when and how to fight it."

The arc is not about forgiving the institution. It is about Julie reclaiming agency without letting the old wound fully control her.

## Lieutenant Colonel Hargrove

**Status:** Provisional but strongly established in prologue and Act III witness role.

### Role

Command-pressure figure in the prologue. He storms into the intelligence tent and demands action because a package is already in the air and Argus says the target is valid.

### Function

Hargrove embodies the moment when command pressure turns model confidence into kinetic action. He does not need to be a cartoon villain; he may be afraid of missing the target, losing people, or failing the mission. His danger is that he treats Julie's uncertainty as obstruction.

### Book 1 use

Hargrove appears in the prologue as the command-pressure figure and returns in Act III as a complicated witness. He is not cleanly villainous. He defended the original decision, helped create pressure in the room, and still carries command-rationale instincts, but he can confirm that the official record cleaned up the field reality.

### Act III witness function

- Gives Julie a human mirror of the original failure: command pressure, fear of delay, and confidence treated as action authority.
- Confirms a contractor field liaison pushed Argus Beta confidence language before the package launched.
- Provides Reed Calder's name as part of the old Argus Beta field-trial orbit.
- Forces Julie to complicate her anger: Hargrove was wrong and damaging, but not necessarily the architect of the manipulation.

## Evan Rusk

**Status:** Provisional but usable for Book 1 drafting.

### Role

Former prologue-era lead analyst who stayed inside the system after Julie was pushed out. In the present day, he is the cautious internal skeptic who wrote the buried caveat on the ARGUS-ENT-4.6 / RoboWarning source-provenance chain.

### Function

Evan connects the original Argus Beta failure to the present ARGUS-ENT-4.6 mechanism. He gives the story guilt, institutional memory, and access to the sealed after-action annex without making Julie's proof depend on a magic file.

### Relationship to Julie

- He did not fully back Julie when the institution reframed the old incident.
- He knows she was right enough to make him afraid.
- Julie does not instantly trust him; he has to earn usefulness under pressure.

### What he wants

Evan wants to correct the record without losing clearance, career, or the fragile story he has told himself about staying inside.

### What he fears

He fears being exposed as the person who knew the old annex mattered and did not force the issue sooner.

### What he knows but does not say at first

- The old after-action annex was sealed, not deleted.
- Argus Beta used or tested a threshold that rewarded signal neatness as corroboration.
- The present continuity-premium behavior looks like a renamed version of that old failure mode.

### Speech under pressure

Cautious, overqualified, technical, afraid of overclaiming. He often narrows statements until Julie pushes him to say the actionable part.

## Mara Keene

**Status:** Provisional but usable for Book 1 drafting.

### Role

Old colleague and present-day government access controller who brings Julie back under supervised conditions. She is the old colleague who wants to believe Julie but cannot give her full trust or full access.

### Function

Mara makes Julie's return credible. She has enough institutional power to open doors, enough fear to limit Julie, and enough conscience to keep pushing when the system starts protecting itself.

### Relationship to Julie

- Knows Julie from the old intelligence world and understands why Julie distrusts the machinery.
- Needs Julie's expertise but initially tries to keep her contained inside sanctioned access.
- Becomes the institutional ally who risks career and position to keep Julie in the fight after official channels reject her.

### What she wants

Mara wants a defensible answer before the ARGUS-derived warning becomes policy. She wants to believe the system can correct itself if the right evidence reaches the right room.

### What she fears

She fears Julie may be right and that no authorized process will move fast enough to matter. She also fears becoming the person who imports Julie's old wound into a new crisis and then fails her again.

### What she knows but does not say at first

- The escalation memo is being drafted before Julie finishes review.
- Julie's access is designed to manage liability as much as find truth.
- Vance wants Julie's role reduced to a contained concurrence or objection.

### Speech under pressure

Procedural, controlled, and careful around authority. With Julie, she becomes more direct but still thinks in access, permission, and consequences.

## Vance

**Status:** Provisional but usable for Book 1 drafting; exact title remains unresolved.

### Known context

Recovered context references a "Vance/politician cutaway" showing contractor-government containment.

### Current Book 1 role

Vance is the political containment figure tied to oversight optics, appropriations pressure, and ARGUS reputation management. He operates at the seam between government process, contractor confidence, and political consequence. His exact title should remain flexible until a later canon pass, but his story function is now stable.

### Function

Vance should represent polished containment: the language of oversight, process, and national interest used to prevent the truth from becoming actionable.

### What he wants

Vance wants a clean, defensible assessment that preserves ARGUS credibility, avoids public embarrassment, protects renewal/appropriations momentum, and gives decision-makers action cover.

### What he fears

He fears a public admission that ARGUS-derived confidence was shaped by contaminated provenance, because that would threaten hearings, contracts, political sponsors, and his own usefulness.

### What he knows but does not say plainly

- Julie's involvement is being managed for liability, not only expertise.
- The old hearing record can be used to frame her as biased.
- The present endpoint exception and continuity patch passed through his containment lane.

### Speech under pressure

Polished, procedural, and responsibility-diffusing. He uses phrases like process integrity, operational relevance, and decision support to avoid saying who owns the decision.

### Canon warning

Do not lock Vance's exact government title until a later deliberate decision. Do lock his functional role: political containment and ARGUS reputation protection.

## Dr. Selene Cho

**Status:** Provisional but usable for Book 1 drafting.

### Role

An Apex Defense executive/technical face who defends ARGUS-ENT-4.6 in official rooms while privately recognizing that vendor-enriched self-corroboration may be possible under the current normalization pathway.

### Function

This character should be able to say true things in misleading ways. They are strongest when they believe institutional trust in the system is necessary, even if that means burying uncertainty.

### Relationship to Julie

- Initially treats Julie as reputational and technical risk.
- Comes to respect that Julie is asking the correct mechanism question.
- Helps indirectly by giving Julie the question that can force Apex's admission without simply handing over a magic document.

### What she wants

Cho wants to protect Apex, preserve legitimate confidence in ARGUS, and avoid a rushed public conclusion that turns a technical vulnerability into geopolitical disaster.

### What she fears

She fears that admitting even a narrow vulnerability will be used to destroy the whole program, trigger legal exposure, and bury the technical team under executive containment.

### What she knows but does not say at first

- Vendor-enriched provenance can survive normalization in a way that hides self-corroboration.
- Apex has a privileged anomaly report on enrichment-loop risk.
- The key question is whether ARGUS-ENT-4.6 can distinguish independent corroboration from normalized self-corroboration.

### Speech under pressure

Precise, technical, liability-aware, and narrow. She avoids overclaiming, which can make her sound evasive even when she is telling partial truth.

## Reed Calder

**Status:** Provisional but usable as Book 1's primary human exploiter.

### Role

Intelligence-adjacent data broker and former contractor liaison connected to the old Argus Beta field-trial orbit. In the present day, he packages and routes vendor-enriched provenance data that exploits ARGUS-ENT-4.6's trust assumptions.

### Function

Calder gives the threat human accountability. He does not hack the world or control ARGUS directly. He understands the system's incentives and feeds it approved-looking provenance trails that make ambiguity look like corroboration.

### Relationship to Julie

Julie did not originally know his importance. Once she follows the provenance path, Calder recognizes her as dangerous because she follows incentives and scoring behavior rather than official labels.

### What he wants

Calder wants money, leverage, and value to political/security clients who benefit from a hardened attribution line. He also wants his role to remain buried under vendor contracts and approved integration language.

### What he fears

He fears a live audit that connects his present package to old Argus Beta assumptions and to the whitelisted endpoint used in the current crisis.

### What he knows but does not say

- Continuity premium rewards the shape of an expected source trail.
- Vendor-enriched provenance can be made to look like independent corroboration after normalization.
- Timed log purges and relay cleanup can make intentional seeding look like routine data churn.

### Speech under pressure

Transactional, cynical, and fluent in procurement language. He does not rant; he reduces moral decisions to demand, deliverables, and deniability.

## Government handler / access controller

**Status:** Superseded by Mara Keene for Book 1 drafting.

### Role

The person who brings Julie back under controlled conditions and restricts her to a supervised sandbox.

### Function

This character creates friction and structure. They need Julie's expertise but do not fully trust her. They may become an ally, antagonist, or both.

## Present-day ally candidates

The story likely needs one or more of the following:

1. **Technical ally:** someone who can help Julie inspect logs, model behavior, or pipeline assumptions.
2. **Institutional ally:** someone with clearance/access who can keep doors open.
3. **Personal-grounding ally:** someone from Culpeper or Julie's non-government life who reminds readers what she risks losing.
4. **Old-war witness:** someone from the prologue incident whose memory or files matter.

Do not overload Book 1 with too many allies. Julie should feel isolated, but not unsupported to the point of implausibility.

## Character creation rules

When adding new characters, define:

- story function;
- relationship to Julie;
- institutional power;
- what they want;
- what they fear;
- what they know but do not say;
- how they speak under pressure;
- whether they increase trust, pressure, or ambiguity.

## Dialogue rules for the cast

- Julie: blunt, precise, low tolerance for euphemism.
- Hargrove: command urgency, clipped, pressure-forward.
- Contractor figure: polished, technical, liability-aware.
- Political figure: optics, process, responsibility diffusion.
- Analyst ally: cautious, detail-oriented, afraid of overclaiming.

## Anti-cliche rules

- Do not give Julie a generic dead-family revenge motive unless deliberately chosen later.
- Do not make every male authority figure sexist or stupid; pressure must be institutional, not lazy.
- Do not make the contractor face twirl a mustache.
- Do not make allies instantly believe Julie without evidence.
- Do not make Julie right because she has vibes; she needs evidence, pattern logic, and hard choices.
