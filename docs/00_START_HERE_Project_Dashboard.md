# 00 — Start Here: Project Dashboard

## Purpose

This repository exists to make the Julie O'Donnell Series writable by humans and AI/coding agents without losing canon, tone, or commercial focus.

The immediate goal is not to draft random prose. The immediate goal is to lock the foundation for a strong Book 1 relaunch: premise, protagonist engine, threat model, canon rules, chapter workflow, and repeatable prompts.

## Current operating truth

Julie O'Donnell is a former U.S. Army intelligence captain whose career broke after she correctly recognized that an AI-enabled targeting/SIGINT confidence pattern was engineered. Six years later, she is trying to live outside government systems in Culpeper, Virginia, but a scaled-up version of the same failure threatens to misclassify a geopolitical event and trigger escalation.

## Commercial lane

This should read like a grounded intelligence thriller with a modern AI/escalation hook. The sell is not "AI is evil." The sell is:

> What happens when a national-security system learns to sound certain, the contractor needs it to be right, command needs it to be actionable, and the only person who knows it can lie is the woman they already ruined for saying so?

## Core reader promise

Each book should deliver:

1. A concrete operational crisis.
2. A system that appears objective but hides human incentives.
3. Julie seeing a pattern before institutions can admit it.
4. A race against escalation, cover-up, or institutional self-protection.
5. Emotional fallout from being right in systems built to punish dissent.

## Do not do yet

- Do not draft the entire book from a thin outline.
- Do not invent a final title and treat it as canon.
- Do not make the AI sentient unless a later canon decision explicitly changes the series premise.
- Do not turn Julie into a generic action hero.
- Do not make the military/government world cartoonishly stupid.
- Do not make contractors pure villains without plausible incentives and deniability.
- Do not flatten Julie into PTSD symptoms; her competence must be visible.
