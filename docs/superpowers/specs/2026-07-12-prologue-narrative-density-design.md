## Objective

Update the Julie O’Donnell house style and revise the Prologue so the manuscript uses fewer low-value dialogue turns, fewer isolated one-sentence paragraphs, and less printed-page whitespace without weakening confrontation, suspense, evidence precision, or emotional force.

The revision must make the prose more narratively compressed, not narrator-dominated. Dialogue remains the preferred vehicle for conflict, authority, deception, moral pressure, and irreversible choice. Narrative carries routine information transfer, already-settled technical facts, repeated qualifications, and nonconsequential status checks.

## Style Guide v2.1 Additions

### Narrative-density standard

Use dialogue when it performs at least one of these functions:

- creates conflict;
- asserts or challenges authority;
- forces a decision;
- reveals deception or divided loyalty;
- exposes moral pressure;
- changes custody, force, evidence, or operational status;
- lands an emotional wound that narrative summary would weaken.

Use narrative when dialogue would only:

- transfer routine information both characters understand;
- repeat a technical distinction already established in the scene;
- list route, injury, evidence, or timing status without changing action;
- qualify the same fact for a third time;
- rehearse minor alternatives after the central choice is clear;
- create question-and-answer rhythm without resistance or consequence.

### Dialogue compression test

For each exchange, ask:

1. Does the answer create conflict, surprise, cost, or a changed decision?
2. Does the speaker’s wording reveal character or institutional position?
3. Would narrative summary preserve the same information with less page space?
4. Has this distinction already been dramatized once?

If the exchange fails the first two tests and passes the third or fourth, compress it into narrative.

### Paragraph-density standard

Isolated one-sentence paragraphs are reserved for:

- discoveries;
- reversals;
- system transitions;
- physical failures;
- moral refusals;
- irreversible actions;
- chapter turns;
- lines whose silence or visual isolation creates necessary pressure.

Low-pressure observations, minor reactions, routine movement, and supporting qualifications should normally be grouped into fuller paragraphs.

No fixed dialogue percentage controls the manuscript. Dramatic function controls.

### Page-density audit

Revision validation must review both word count and page-shape indicators:

- number of dialogue turns;
- number of isolated one-sentence paragraphs;
- fragment frequency;
- repeated screen-text translation;
- repeated evidentiary qualification;
- paragraph density outside action sequences.

The goal is not uniformly long paragraphs. The goal is contrast: dense narrative during setup and analysis, tighter spacing during crisis and irreversible choice.

## Prologue Revision Design

### Dominant dramatic mission

Julie must prove the target feed is synthetic and stop the strike before release; after the strike, she must preserve the causal truth against an official record that erases the four-second gateway delay.

### Structural approach

Preserve the existing three-part architecture:

1. Intelligence-tent anomaly and confrontation.
2. Abort delay, strike, and immediate causal record.
3. Investigation, Marcus’s defensible testimony, and Julie’s resignation.

Do not split the Prologue.

### Dialogue to preserve

Preserve or closely retain the exchanges that create conflict or lasting character definition:

- Hargrove demanding the holdup.
- Julie’s statement that the feed is engineered or too perfect.
- The uncertainty argument between Julie and Hargrove.
- Julie demanding sixty seconds.
- Marcus’s late instruction to abort.
- The emergency abort transmission.
- Marcus saying, “We all tried.”
- Julie challenging the hearing timeline.
- Marcus saying, “There was confusion in the room.”
- The Fort Belvoir confrontation ending with, “You did what you could defend.”

Minor wording may change for rhythm, but the dramatic function and moral meaning must remain.

### Dialogue to compress

Convert or reduce:

- Baines confirming how many times the sequence has run.
- Repetitive encryption/movement/thermal rebuttal pairs once the physical contradiction is established.
- Routine cross-check timing exchanges that do not alter the decision.
- Repeated hearing questions after the four-second omission is clear.
- Explanations following Marcus’s “confusion” testimony.
- Any question-and-answer sequence that restates a fact the narration has already demonstrated.

### Technical facts that must remain exact

- 11.2-second packet spacing.
- 94.1 percent Argus confidence.
- Weather degradation affecting other sources.
- Constant target signal.
- Line-of-sight/terrain contradiction.
- Exercise 7C carrier-noise match.
- Synthetic noise generated at Fort Belvoir.
- Abort initiated at 09:41:16.
- Weapon release at 09:41:19.
- Gateway authentication completed at 09:41:20.
- Eleven civilians killed, including five children.
- Imran Khalid misidentified as Farid Anwar.
- Anwar’s real convoy escaping.
- Original local cache overwritten.
- Julie cleared while the report assigns analyst delay.
- Hargrove receives only a nonpermanent letter of concern.
- Argus Beta remains operational.
- Julie resigns three months later.

### Emotional spine

The emotional progression must remain:

1. Julie trusts physical evidence over certified confidence.
2. Hargrove converts uncertainty into permission to act.
3. Marcus supports Julie only when the decision is almost irreversible.
4. Argus delays the abort by four seconds.
5. The surviving record converts system delay into analyst delay.
6. Marcus protects himself through defensible wording.
7. Julie leaves because the institution chooses the record it can survive.

### Paragraph-rhythm strategy

- Use fuller paragraphs during the initial technical comparison.
- Shorten paragraphs as release criteria approaches.
- Preserve isolated time beats during the four-second gateway delay.
- Restore fuller narrative paragraphs during casualty confirmation and the investigation summary.
- Isolate “There was confusion in the room” and the final institutional judgment.

### Target reduction

- Target approximately 10–15 percent fewer words.
- Seek a larger proportional reduction in dialogue turns and isolated paragraphs.
- Do not cut atmosphere, causality, casualty consequence, or the resignation scene merely to reach a number.

## Continuity and Canon Constraints

The revision must not:

- identify the current conspiracy as responsible for the six-years-ago event;
- create a surviving copy of the overwritten local cache;
- make Marcus support Julie decisively before the final seconds;
- change the four-second sequence;
- reduce the civilian casualty count;
- make Argus sentient or knowingly deceptive;
- absolve Hargrove or Marcus;
- imply Julie was formally punished for misconduct;
- alter the opening state of Chapter 1 or the six-year separation.

## Validation

Before acceptance:

1. Compare the revised Prologue with the current Prologue line by line for canon preservation.
2. Compare the ending with Chapter 1’s references to the strike, report, Marcus, resignation, nightmares, and the phrase “analyst delay.”
3. Confirm all three exact timestamps remain unchanged.
4. Confirm the current conspiracy remains unproved in relation to the old strike.
5. Confirm the final three-line ending remains intact or functionally identical.
6. Count words, dialogue turns, isolated one-sentence paragraphs, and fragments before and after.
7. Confirm no living-author imitation or borrowed stylistic mannerisms.

The revision succeeds when:

- the Prologue reads faster without feeling summarized;
- dialogue carries only conflict, choice, authority, or emotional injury;
- technical facts remain exact and legible;
- the four-second abort sequence retains maximum visual pressure;
- the hearing and resignation remain emotionally sharp;
- page density improves outside the crisis sequence;
- Chapter 1 requires no continuity repair.
