## Objective

The chapter must remain narratively compressed rather than narrator-dominated. Dialogue stays where it creates conflict, authority, suspicion, fear, exposure, or changed action. ## Dramatic Architecture

### Dominant dramatic mission

Julie must establish that the Pakistan feed violates the physical world and locate its synthetic source while Elias independently discovers that his archived validation package has entered production under a forged record of his authorization.

### Preserved structure

The chapter remains one chapter with two controlled threads:

1. **Julie / Room 402B:** physical anomaly → restricted archive → Validation Package 88 → hidden evidence path → containment.
2. **Elias / integration lab:** duplicate-object warning → production discovery → forged authentication → evidence copy → administrative detention.
3. **Return to Julie:** similarity becomes a stronger but still incomplete chain → hidden capture → locked-room endpoint.

Do not split the chapter. The parallel structure is the engine: Julie discovers the package from outside while Elias discovers the deployment from inside.

## Three Approaches Considered

### 1. Light line edit

Preserve almost every exchange and paragraph, trimming only repeated words.

**Trade-off:** Lowest continuity risk but insufficient page-density improvement for a 6,700-word chapter with several repeated explanation ladders.

### 2. Moderate narrative compression — selected

Preserve all discoveries, system states, adversarial exchanges, and countdown beats. Convert routine explanation, development history, repeated evidentiary qualification, and unchanged status dialogue into specific narrative.

**Trade-off:** Requires a full-chapter rewrite but provides the best balance of speed, clarity, and emotional continuity.

### 3. Strong structural compression

Shorten the technical analysis and Payload 88 history aggressively, potentially reducing or relocating one POV segment.

**Trade-off:** Greater page savings but unacceptable risk to Elias’s framing, Julie’s proof method, and the parallel architecture.
