## Objective

Create Narrative House Style v2.2 and apply a second line-density pass to Chapters 1 and 2. The work should reduce printed-page inflation caused by dialogue ladders, isolated reactions, routine screen fields, transfer-status commentary, and one-line narrative fragments while preserving the procedural-intelligence identity, emotional conflicts, exact evidence limits, and action/countdown rhythm.

This is not a plot rewrite. It is a page-shape and narrative-delivery revision.

## Approaches considered

### A. Preserve v2.1 as final

Retain the current chapters and accept the remaining dialogue-heavy page shape.

**Advantage:** No further continuity risk.  
**Disadvantage:** Chapters 1 and 2 still contain more dialogue starts and isolated paragraphs than a traditionally narrative thriller requires.

### B. Narrative consolidation second pass — approved

Convert routine dialogue and low-pressure fragments into close-third narrative while retaining exchanges that create conflict, authority, evidence, deception, moral cost, or irreversible action.

**Advantage:** Reduces line count and printed-page use without materially shortening the story.  
**Disadvantage:** Requires disciplined protection of character voice and evidence precision.

### C. Strong narrator-dominant rewrite

Convert most technical and procedural exchanges into summary.

**Advantage:** Largest possible page reduction.  
**Disadvantage:** Flattens Julie–Marcus conflict, Elias’s fear, Sarah’s institutional positioning, and the procedural suspense that distinguishes the manuscript.

## House Style v2.2 rules

### Dialogue-chain review

Any chain of more than three short dialogue turns must be reviewed.

Retain the chain only when each turn does at least one of the following:

- escalates conflict;
- narrows an option;
- changes authority or custody;
- creates a record;
- forces a concession;
- reveals deception or divided loyalty;
- advances a countdown, authentication, or physical struggle;
- lands a defining emotional wound.

Otherwise retain the strongest line or two and carry the settled information in narrative.

### Question-to-exposition rule

A character should not ask a sequence of questions merely to cue technical explanation. After the first consequential challenge, use close-third narrative to summarize routine answers, rejected alternatives, unchanged status, and already-established limitations.

### Screen-field density rule

Use an isolated screen block only when exact wording, order, timing, custody, or authority changes the decision.

Routine fields should be incorporated into prose, for example:

> Fingerprint validation and token challenge both showed CONFIRMED. The workstation source was AMD-LAB-07—the desk in front of Elias.

Preserve isolated display treatment for:

- the three Package 88 safeguards;
- `PAK_RELAY_17A_SOURCE_CORRECTION`;
- the 02:14 false authorization and `APX-DIR-0019`;
- major access, custody, or authority changes;
- remote sanitization and evidence-capture state changes;
- the 85-percent/deadbolt endpoint;
- any field whose exact wording becomes later evidence.

### Paragraph-joining rule

Join low-pressure one-sentence paragraphs when they carry:

- routine movement;
- a minor reaction;
- unchanged injury or surveillance status;
- atmosphere without an operational effect;
- explanatory paraphrase after a clear line;
- a transfer percentage that does not force a new choice.

Do not join paragraphs when isolation creates necessary pressure during:

- discoveries;
- reversals;
- authentication;
- containment;
- physical failure;
- moral refusal;
- chapter endings.

### Indirect-dialogue rule

Use indirect or summarized dialogue when exact wording does not become evidence or define character. Preserve direct dialogue for authority, betrayal, uncertainty, accountability, refusal, and relationship wounds.

### Count target

This second pass should prioritize physical-line reduction over word reduction.

Target for each chapter:

- approximately 3–7 percent fewer words;
- approximately 10–18 percent fewer physical Markdown lines;
- fewer dialogue-start paragraphs;
- fewer isolated one-sentence paragraphs outside crisis sequences.

These are targets, not quotas.
