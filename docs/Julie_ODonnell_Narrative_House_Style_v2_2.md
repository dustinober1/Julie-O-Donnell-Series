# 1. Second-pass principle

A first narrative-density pass removes obvious repetition. A second pass asks whether the surviving page still behaves like a novel or like a screenplay carrying technical explanation.

The second-pass goal is not to eliminate dialogue. It is to make the page alternate deliberately between:

- denser close-third narrative for analysis, routine information transfer, settled possibilities, and procedural context;
- direct dialogue for authority, conflict, deception, accountability, moral pressure, and changed decisions;
- short paragraphs for countdowns, discoveries, reversals, physical failure, and irreversible action.

A quiet page should not use the same fragmented shape as a breach, capture, authentication, or escape.

---

# 2. Dialogue-chain rule

Any chain of more than three short dialogue turns must be reviewed.

Keep the full chain only when each turn:

- escalates conflict;
- narrows an option;
- changes authority, force, custody, or timing;
- creates a reviewable record;
- forces a concession;
- reveals a lie, divided loyalty, or responsibility;
- advances a countdown, authentication, or physical struggle;
- lands a defining emotional wound.

Compress the chain when later turns merely:

- confirm information already understood;
- list predictable alternatives;
- repeat an evidence limitation;
- cue exposition;
- restate an unchanged status;
- paraphrase a screen field;
- provide conversational rhythm without new pressure.

## Preferred pattern

Preserve the controlling exchange:

> “Could he have injected it?”  
> “He built a test package. That is not the same fact.”

Then summarize settled similarities in narrative:

> The scenario, interval, geography, and likely carrier profile all aligned. They established resemblance, not deployment.

Do not continue the exchange merely to speak each similarity aloud.

---

# 3. Question-to-exposition rule

A character should not ask several questions whose only purpose is to let another character teach the reader.

After the first consequential challenge:

- summarize routine answers through the POV character’s close-third understanding;
- preserve only the limitation that changes the next action;
- return to direct dialogue when someone resists, overclaims, refuses, or chooses.

## Appropriate direct questioning

Julie asks questions to:

- expose missing proof;
- identify who controls a record or door;
- force someone to own an order;
- prevent an overclaim;
- define an abort condition;
- assign responsibility.

She should not ask a ladder of questions merely to retrieve configuration, route, or evidence facts the narration can deliver more efficiently.

---

# 4. Indirect-dialogue rule

Use indirect dialogue when exact wording does not become evidence or define character.

Prefer direct dialogue for:

- orders;
- refusals;
- admissions;
- accusations;
- evidence qualifications challenged by another person;
- custody and force language;
- institutional euphemisms that matter later;
- relationship wounds;
- lines that change the operation.

Prefer indirect or summarized dialogue for:

- routine status reports;
- minor logistics;
- repeated rejected options;
- settled technical explanations;
- questions whose answers do not surprise or change action;
- conversation that merely confirms the narration.

Indirect dialogue must remain specific. It should identify who said what mattered, what was withheld, and what consequence followed.

---

# 5. Screen-field density

Exact screen text remains one of the series’ strongest tools when wording, order, time, custody, or authority matters.

Use an isolated screen block when it:

- establishes an exact timestamp;
- names a person, service, device, or authority;
- changes access, force, custody, or system state;
- creates a later evidentiary distinction;
- shows an irreversible transition;
- drives a countdown or physical decision;
- contradicts the public or official account.

Integrate routine fields into prose when the same fact can be preserved without losing evidentiary meaning.

Instead of:

> FINGERPRINT VALIDATION: CONFIRMED  
> TOKEN CHALLENGE: CONFIRMED  
> WORKSTATION SOURCE: AMD-LAB-07

Use:

> Fingerprint validation and token challenge both showed CONFIRMED. The workstation source was AMD-LAB-07—the desk in front of Elias.

Preserve isolated display treatment for:

- the three Package 88 safeguards;
- `PAK_RELAY_17A_SOURCE_CORRECTION`;
- `APX-DIR-0019`;
- exact evidence-capture and sanitization states;
- exact sponsor, access, or custody changes;
- the 85-percent/deadbolt handoff;
- any field later quoted, challenged, or used to establish sequence.

---

# 6. Paragraph-joining rule

Join a one-sentence paragraph to an adjacent paragraph when it carries:

- routine movement;
- a minor reaction;
- unchanged surveillance or injury status;
- atmosphere without operational effect;
- explanatory paraphrase after the beat has landed;
- a transfer percentage that does not change the available choice;
- a familiar theme stated again without a new consequence.

Keep a sentence isolated when white space creates necessary pressure around:

- discovery;
- reversal;
- recognition;
- authentication;
- physical failure;
- moral refusal;
- system transition;
- exact time;
- irreversible action;
- chapter turn.

## Contrast rule

The value of a short paragraph comes from contrast. A page made entirely of short paragraphs eliminates that contrast and causes ordinary observations to compete visually with decisive facts.

---

# 7. Dialogue tags and beats

When two speakers are clear, avoid giving every line its own reaction beat.

Use beats when they:

- change physical position;
- expose fear, injury, hesitation, or control;
- affect evidence handling;
- reveal surveillance or force geometry;
- create silence with meaning.

Remove or combine beats that merely report looking, turning, nodding, or pausing without changing the scene.

---

# 8. Quiet-scene density

During analysis, planning, travel, intake, debriefing, and evidence review:

- favor paragraphs containing two to five related sentences;
- allow one paragraph to carry a routine exchange and its operational meaning;
- introduce an object when it constrains action, not through repeated inventory;
- summarize lesser alternatives after dramatizing the two strongest;
- keep the POV close by showing how the information changes attention or choice.

A denser paragraph must not become an encyclopedia entry. It should still move toward a threat, decision, contradiction, or cost.

---

# 9. Crisis exception

Do not apply quiet-scene density uniformly to:

- authentication;
- transfer countdowns;
- containment;
- breaches;
- stairwells and vehicle escapes;
- force-standard confrontations;
- evidence sealing;
- moral refusals;
- chapter endings.

During crisis:

- short lines may accelerate perception;
- isolated fields may establish order;
- one-line dialogue may expose conflicting command;
- fragments may mark failure or irreversible choice.

Second-pass compression may remove repeated commentary between states, but it must not blur the sequence of those states.

# 12. Second-pass quantitative audit

For every second-pass chapter, record:

- pre-pass and post-pass word count;
- pre-pass and post-pass physical Markdown lines;
- pre-pass and post-pass dialogue-start paragraphs;
- pre-pass and post-pass isolated one-sentence paragraphs;
- major direct dialogue deliberately preserved;
- major exchanges converted to narrative;
- crisis sections exempted from consolidation.

Desired pattern:

- word count decreases modestly;
- physical lines decrease more substantially;
- dialogue starts decrease;
- quiet sections become denser;
- action sections retain visual speed.

A chapter does not pass merely because it is shorter. It passes when page shape reflects dramatic intensity.

---

# 13. Final second-pass check

Before accepting a chapter, answer:

1. Does any dialogue chain exceed three turns without escalating pressure?
2. Can any question ladder become one close-third paragraph?
3. Is every isolated screen block evidentially or operationally necessary?
4. Does every one-line narrative paragraph deserve emphasis?
5. Have routine reactions been combined without losing interiority?
6. Are the strongest relationship and authority lines still direct?
7. Does action remain spatially and mechanically clear?
8. Did line reduction occur mainly outside crisis sequences?
9. Are all evidence and knowledge limitations unchanged?
10. Does the prose remain distinctly Julie O’Donnell rather than resembling another author?
