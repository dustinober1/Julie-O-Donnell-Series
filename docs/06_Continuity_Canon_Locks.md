# 06 — Continuity Canon Locks

## Purpose

This file prevents drift. Agents must treat these rules as controlling unless the user explicitly changes canon.

## Source hierarchy

1. User-stated canon in the current repo or current chat.
2. `PROJECT_STATE.yaml`.
3. Story bible documents under `docs/`.
4. Outlines under `books/book-01/`.
5. Draft prose.

If prose contradicts canon docs, flag the contradiction before editing.

## Locked core facts

### Protagonist

- The protagonist is Julie O'Donnell.
- She is a former U.S. Army Captain.
- She worked in intelligence.
- She was involved in a six-years-ago incident at a forward operating base near the Pakistan border.
- She now lives in or near Culpeper, Virginia.
- Her present life includes farmhouse/property routines, horses, and fence work.

### Prologue incident

- The prologue takes place six years before the main story.
- Julie is still Captain Julie O'Donnell in the prologue.
- The setting is a forward operating base near the Pakistan border.
- Julie identifies a suspicious SIGINT/timestamp pattern.
- She says the pattern is engineered / a setup.
- Argus Beta is involved.
- Command pressure is intense.
- Lieutenant Colonel Hargrove pressures Julie to stop delaying action.
- The system confidence is high enough that others treat action as justified.

### Present-day premise

- A later ARGUS/Apex system family issue pulls Julie back toward government/intelligence work.
- The present-day system has scaled beyond the prologue version.
- A misclassification or attribution failure may contribute to geopolitical escalation involving Pakistan and India.
- Julie is needed because of her prior experience and pattern-recognition skill.
- Julie is not fully trusted when she is brought back in.
- Her access may be restricted to a supervised sandbox.

### System naming

- Apex Defense is the contractor name currently in use.
- ARGUS / Argus is the system family.
- Argus Beta is the prologue-era system.
- ARGUS-ENT-4.6 is the present enterprise system.
- RoboWarning is provisional and must be clarified later.

## Thematic locks

- The series is about outsourced certainty and human accountability.
- The AI should not be treated as a sentient villain.
- The danger comes from the interaction of data, incentives, command pressure, and institutional self-protection.
- Julie's wound is not that she was wrong. Her wound is that being right did not save her or the people affected by the decision.
- Julie must remain competent.
- Institutions must be believable, not cartoonishly evil.

## Julie portrayal locks

Julie can be damaged, angry, guarded, and isolated, but she cannot be written as incompetent, hysterical, or merely lucky.

Julie should demonstrate competence through:

- specific observations;
- disciplined reasoning;
- operational memory;
- willingness to challenge clean narratives;
- ability to separate evidence from fear;
- ability to see institutional pressure as part of the analytic environment.

## Villain / antagonist locks

Until changed, do not make the AI itself the mastermind. Use human antagonists, institutional incentives, political pressure, and contractor self-protection.

A strong antagonist may:

- manipulate data inputs;
- conceal uncertainty;
- exploit confidence thresholds;
- weaponize Julie's reputation;
- accelerate decision timelines;
- frame caution as weakness.

## Realism locks

- Avoid omnipotent hacking.
- Avoid one-click proof.
- Avoid a lone analyst instantly overruling national policy.
- Avoid acronyms without context.
- Avoid action scenes that disconnect from the intelligence-thriller premise.
- Avoid explaining model mechanics at textbook length.

## Continuity watchlist

Before any chapter draft is accepted, check:

1. Does Julie act with visible expertise?
2. Does the system output have a clear operational consequence?
3. Is there a plausible human reason someone resists Julie's warning?
4. Does the scene increase pressure?
5. Does any new tech detail contradict the ARGUS naming model?
6. Does the chapter preserve the prologue wound?
7. Does the chapter avoid making Julie's skepticism purely emotional?
8. Does the chapter maintain thriller momentum?
