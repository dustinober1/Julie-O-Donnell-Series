## Representative review candidates

The strongest remaining pattern is the routine action or reaction isolated as its own paragraph:

- “Marcus paused.”
- “He hesitated.”
- “Marcus nodded.”
- “Elias did not answer.”
- “Vance folded his hands.”
- “Elias looked at the confession.”
- “Marcus stopped pacing.”
- “Elias stopped.”
- “Bell stared at her.”
- “A short pause.”
- “Marcus waited.”
- “He pressed it.”

Other candidates are isolated technical transitions that do not always need their own visual emphasis:

- “The speaker crackled.”
- “The console chimed.”
- “The wheel stopped.”
- “The display updated.”
- “The clock showed 00:04.”
- “The first line appeared.”

Some short lines remain justified and should be protected, including recurring motifs, major reversals, critical countdown beats, scene endpoints, and the final line.

## Recommended repair scope

Do **not** perform another indiscriminate manuscript-wide merge. Apply a human-reviewed targeted pass to the **230 flagged paragraphs**, prioritizing Chapters 4, 5, 1, 12, 13, 8, 14, and 10. Integrate routine reactions and technical transitions into neighboring prose while preserving dialogue, countdown pressure, system displays, scene boundaries, and intentional emphasis.
