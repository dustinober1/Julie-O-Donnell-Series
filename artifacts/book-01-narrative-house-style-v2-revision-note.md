## Why this guide was created

The accepted manuscript already has a strong original identity built around procedural intelligence, physical causality, evidentiary restraint, institutional pressure, and moral responsibility under uncertainty.

The manuscript’s principal style risk is not excessive intelligence or technical detail. It is repeated explanation after the scene has already made the technical or evidentiary distinction clear.

House Style v2.0 was created to:

- increase forward movement;
- enter scenes closer to the active problem;
- make technical explanation arise from visible effect and forced choice;
- preserve human pressure inside procedural scenes;
- reduce repeated evidence qualifications, injury reminders, countdowns, and screen-text paraphrase;
- strengthen dominant scene and chapter missions;
- protect consequential chapter endings;
- retain the manuscript’s institutional realism and evidence limits;
- prevent future drafting from imitating the recognizable prose of any living author.

## Originality control

The style guide permits only high-level thriller craft principles. It expressly prohibits imitation, paraphrase, or close emulation of the recognizable prose, rhythm, imagery, scene construction, or stylistic signatures of any living author.

The Julie O’Donnell voice must continue to arise from its own manuscript, canon, characters, moral problem, and physical/institutional world.
