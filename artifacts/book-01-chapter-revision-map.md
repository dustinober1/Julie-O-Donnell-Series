# Book 1 Chapter Revision Map — House Style v2.0

**Status:** Planning artifact only  
**Manuscript effect:** No accepted prose has been revised  
**Accepted scope:** Prologue and Chapters 1–12  
**Baseline:** `66a7aeab21b32dcf64d90ce313dd9c710ca311c3`

## Use

This map identifies where House Style v2.0 should be applied in a later, separately authorized revision pass.

It does not authorize silent continuity changes, chapter renumbering, new plot information, changed evidence strength, repaired injuries, altered POV knowledge, or changes to the locked Chapter 13 mission.

Approximate lengths are rounded from the accepted Markdown manuscript and are intended for revision planning, not publication metadata. Short screen-text lines and Markdown spacing make exact counts vary from tracker totals.

## Revision intensity definitions

- **Light:** Preserve architecture and most prose; tighten repetition, paragraph endings, and scene entry.
- **Moderate:** Rebuild selected passages or scene transitions while preserving chapter structure.
- **Heavy:** Reassess scene architecture, dominant mission, and substantial blocks of exposition; preserve all accepted events and canon.

## Recommended revision sequence

The recommended sequence is based on leverage and continuity risk, not reading order.

# Prologue — Six Years Ago

## Current dramatic mission

Julie must prove the target feed is synthetic and stop the strike before release; after the strike, she must preserve the causal truth against an official record that erases the four-second gateway delay.

## Approximate length

Approximately **2,450 words**.

## Revision intensity

**Light.**

## Primary pacing problem

The opening analysis is strong. The investigation and hearing aftermath contains several panels and repeated explanations of how the record displaced the causal truth. The sequence can tighten after the four-second log is established.

## Primary character opportunity

Sharpen the distinction between Julie’s technical certainty and Marcus’s delayed moral support. His later “confusion” testimony should remain the emotional knife.

## Technical material that must remain

- Exact 11.2-second spacing.
- Weather and line-of-sight contradictions.
- Exercise 7C carrier-noise match.
- Abort initiated at 09:41:16.
- Weapon release at 09:41:19.
- Gateway authentication completed at 09:41:20.
- Eleven civilian deaths, including five children.
- Imran Khalid misidentified as Farid Anwar.
- Original local cache overwritten.
- Current conspiracy connection unproved.

## Repetition that may be compressed

- Multiple forms of “the system was wrong but certified.”
- Repeated hearing questions about Julie’s timing.
- Explanations after Marcus’s “confusion” answer.
- Some post-strike institutional summary before the resignation scene.

## Natural split point

No recommended split. The prologue’s power comes from connecting the operational failure to the record failure.

## Ending recommendation

**Retain.** The final contrast between machine error and the survivable official record defines the novel.

## Canon and continuity risks

- Do not identify the current conspiracy as responsible.
- Do not add a surviving cache.
- Do not make Marcus fully support Julie before the release.
- Do not change the four-second sequence.
- Do not make Argus sentient or intentionally malicious.

## Revision target

Move more quickly from anomaly to abort, and from hearing distortion to Marcus’s testimony. Trust the final line; do not explain it afterward.
