# Chapter 1 Blueprint — The Official Version

## Chapter Metadata

- Book: Book 1 — Untitled Julie O'Donnell Book 1
- Chapter number: 1
- Working title: The Official Version
- POV: Julie O'Donnell, close third person
- Timeline: Several months after the prologue
- Location: Classified oversight hearing room, Washington, D.C.
- Target word count: 2,400–2,800 words
- Status: Blueprint complete; first draft complete; revision and continuity review pending

## Chapter Purpose

Show the institution converting Julie's accurate warning into an officer-performance failure. This is the second wound of the opening: command first ignored her warning, then the oversight process protects Argus Beta by redefining the dispute as one officer's unsupported hesitation under operational pressure.

The chapter must also establish the record that Chapter 49 will later correct.

## Starting State

- Plot state: Several months after the FOB decision; the incident has been converted into an after-action packet and classified exhibits.
- Julie emotional state: Prepared, controlled, and still expecting precise evidence to matter if she can put it in front of the right people.
- Technical state: The record foregrounds Argus Beta's 91% confidence while minimizing the six-event timing pattern and the confidence rise without new primary evidence.
- Institutional pressure: The hearing's scope favors operational reasonableness, documented software performance, and what Julie could prove before the decision point.

## Required Scene Sequence

### Scene 1 — The Number on the Wall

- Location: Windowless classified hearing room.
- Characters: Julie, hearing chair, board counsel, recorder, program representative.
- Conflict: Julie is asked to confirm that she lacked proof of fabricated intelligence before the target window closed.
- New information: The projected chronology reduces her warning to `dissenting analytical caution` beneath an enlarged 91% confidence score.
- Emotional turn: Julie recognizes that the hearing has begun with its preferred conclusion already formatted.

### Scene 2 — The Order of the Evidence

- Location: Same room, after-action packet projected page by page.
- Characters: Julie, counsel, chair, program representative.
- Conflict: The packet contains the underlying events but groups them by collection source, making the chronological pattern disappear.
- New information: Julie reconstructs the six timestamps—02:14:12 through 02:26:42—and explains that the order and spacing were the evidence of non-independence.
- Emotional turn: Julie briefly forces the room to see the pattern, but the chair narrows the issue to whether she could prove fabrication or identify a manipulator.

### Scene 3 — Within Parameters

- Location: Same room, program-performance portion of the hearing.
- Characters: Julie, chair, program representative, board counsel.
- Conflict: The program representative states that Argus Beta processed the inputs it received and operated within configured or authorized parameters. Julie argues that correct processing does not make dependent inputs independent.
- New information: The hearing separates system performance from source validity, then assigns the unresolved source problem to Julie's analytic judgment.
- Emotional turn: Julie understands that no software defect is required for the system to produce dangerous certainty—and that this distinction is being used to protect the program rather than investigate the warning.

### Scene 4 — The Official Version

- Location: Same room, proposed findings read into the classified record.
- Characters: Julie, chair, recorder.
- Conflict: Julie asks that her actual warning be entered exactly. It is accepted only as a supplemental recollection, while the controlling finding preserves the narrower wording.
- New information: The board's finding treats Argus Beta as functioning within authorized parameters and Julie's warning as an insufficiently supported deviation from the operational assessment.
- Emotional turn: Julie stops trying to win the room and listens closely enough to remember what it chooses to call true.

## Clue Movement

- Old clue recontextualized: The six timestamps and 87→89→91 confidence rise become evidence that can be hidden by presentation and scope rather than erased outright.
- Institutional clue: A record can preserve individual data points while neutralizing their relationship.
- False conclusion: Because Julie could not identify the manipulator in time, her warning is treated as unsupported hesitation.
- Reader understanding: Julie was specific and analytically disciplined; the institution protects Argus Beta through categorization, not an obvious lie.
- What Julie learns: The official version is built from selection, ordering, and procedural scope.

## Technical Movement

- System behavior: Argus Beta reports 91% confidence and multi-source validation from the submitted event chain.
- Data issue: Source-grouped presentation obscures cross-source timing regularity.
- Plausibility note: Keep the hearing focused on source independence, chronology, confidence movement, and decision timing. Do not turn it into a software lecture or a courtroom drama.
- What not to reveal: Every present-day mechanism, actor, and solution named in the context packet's forbidden-reveal section.

## Character Movement

- Julie: Begins believing precise correction can repair the record; ends understanding that the room's scope is designed to convert her precision into a performance issue.
- Hearing chair: Maintains procedural control and allows Julie to speak while restricting what her explanation can change.
- Program representative: Defends the narrow, supportable claim that Argus Beta processed its authorized inputs as configured.
- Institutional antagonist: The recordmaking process itself—reasonable voices, selective exhibits, narrowed questions, and findings that assign uncertainty to the human analyst.

## Ending Hook

The record formally chooses its version. The recorder asks Julie to verify the spelling of her surname; the administrative correction is made while her six-timestamp sequence remains outside the controlling finding. The next chapter can then cut to the present-day life she built after learning what the institution would preserve.
