# Chapter 18 — The Local Record

October 14
18:42 Indian Standard Time / 09:12 Eastern Daylight Time

Forward Post Arjun
Kashmir

Captain Arvind Rao reached the inner gate with one man on a stretcher and a steel transit box chained to his wrist.

Sleet struck the box and the patrol’s white overgarments with the same dry sound. Major Ananya Sharma met them before the pedestrian barrier closed.

“Medical first.”

Lance Naik Suresh Pal lay conscious beneath the straps, a field dressing covering the left side of his scalp. He tried to speak.

“The north track—”

The medical officer cut him off. “I am removing him from questioning.”

Sharma held Pal’s gaze long enough to show she had heard him. “Go.”

The stretcher disappeared into the aid room.

Rao looked at the document pouch against his chest as if the report might need Pal’s voice to remain complete.

“It doesn’t,” Sharma said. “Mark what he personally observed and what he did not. His questioning stops here.”

The fifth patrol member stepped forward. He was older than Rao and wore a relay-maintenance badge inside a clear pocket.

“Naib Subedar Vikram Sethi. K-17 local custodian.”

The box on Rao’s wrist prevented headquarters from turning an intact mast and empty compound into closure.

Sharma kept the intake off the allied network. Lieutenant Sameer Qureshi opened a local incident under the Forward Post Arjun clock. Sethi was transferor and relay custodian. Rao was patrol witness. Qureshi would receive the object. Sharma controlled the incident umbrella. Pal was listed as a witness removed by medical authority before questioning.

Only then did the patrol enter the report room.

Wet outer layers steamed beside a portable heater. Qureshi positioned a camera, paper transfer sheet, and two empty trays beneath the disciplined wall clock.

“Physical sequence,” Sharma told Rao. “No conclusion.”

The patrol had reached Point Kestrel late after ice closed part of the western traverse and one man fell near an anchor line. From Kestrel they observed K-17 for nine minutes. The mast stood. External power looked normal. The weather cover over the service enclosure remained closed. No person occupied the visible outer compound.

At the outer security wicket, frozen mud held six partial approach marks and a deeper cut where a rigid tool had been set against the lower frame. The latch was closed when the patrol arrived, but anti-tamper paint had been shaved from the receiver. The indicator behind the protective glass showed a steady accepted state.

“The patrol report calls it a successful pass,” Sharma said.

“That is the controller’s state for an accepted local challenge,” Sethi replied.

“Not the same sentence.”

“No, ma’am.”

The inner boundary stood twenty meters beyond the wicket, armored into the rock beneath the mast. Its door was closed. Windblown ice obscured the lower seam.

“We did not cross,” Rao said. “We could not exclude hostile occupancy, an explosive or tamper hazard, or a withdrawal route toward the Line of Control.”

The hard stop had done what it was for. The patrol preserved the question rather than converting uncertainty into pursuit.

Sethi explained the transit box. K-17 wrote its append-only local incident chain to a cartridge held in an exterior isolation cassette. The registered local custodian could remove the cartridge from the safe side without opening the relay or creating a network path. Sethi had verified a sealed journal, removed the cartridge with his mechanical key, capped the empty cassette, and isolated the local incident-write path. Rao witnessed each step and photographed the cassette before, during, and after removal.

“Did anyone enter the inner enclosure?” Sharma asked.

“No.”

“Connect equipment to the relay?”

“No.”

“Pursue the tracks?”

“No.”

“What can you say about the people who made them?”

“Only that no one remained visible in the outer compound when we arrived.”

That was the boundary.

Northern Command called for service restoration.

“The local incident cartridge is under preservation intake,” Sharma said. “K-17 remains held.”

“For an intact relay and an empty compound?” the duty colonel asked.

“For an intact relay with an accepted outer challenge, unexplained approach marks, an unresolved inner boundary, and a local record no headquarters product has examined.”

“You have no hostile personnel.”

“We have no hostile personnel in custody.”

The distinction made the delay hers. The colonel ordered her to determine whether the relay had been altered and return it to service as soon as evidence and operational safety allowed. He did not order deletion or reconnection.

Qureshi photographed the unopened box and visible field seal before the chain came off Rao’s wrist. Sethi opened it only after the transfer sheet named the original cartridge, recipient, witnesses, and Arjun’s clock. Inside lay a gray cartridge no larger than a field-radio battery. Its indicator showed JOURNAL SEALED.

The original remained the original. The patrol photographs and report became a separate package. A derivative would exist only if an isolated read succeeded.

Arjun’s relay-failure reader had no radio, network interface, or source-side write conductors. Qureshi first tested it with a reference cartridge carrying six known events. The reader returned all six in order and reported zero source writes.

Sharma stated the limits before the K-17 cartridge entered the source slot.

“No repair. No normalization. No decryption. No inferred fields. Preserve the controller clock and its recorded offset.”

The event chain opened.

K-17’s controller had last checked regional time at 17:08 with an offset of plus 0.482 seconds. Qureshi left the offset attached rather than cleaning the timestamps after the fact.

At 17:11:46 on the local clock, Security Line One received and accepted a field-route challenge. The outer wicket opened and closed.

A certificate followed.

FIELD ROUTE CERTIFICATE: ACCEPTED
REGISTERED AUTHORITY: TARIQ, MAJOR / FORWARD LIAISON CELL
PHYSICAL OPERATOR: NOT RECORDED

Rao looked toward Sharma.

“A registered authority is not a man on the mountain,” she said. “Enter the subject. Do not place Tariq at the relay.”

Qureshi did.

The next event showed the task profile.

K17-PHASE-B
LOCAL CHALLENGE OPEN
FINAL AUTHORIZATION PENDING

The event order crossed a journal segment without a gap. The reader continued to report zero source writes.

At 17:12, the controller received a structured maintenance request.

ACTION CLASS: LOCAL SOURCE-RECORD RECONSTRUCTION
SUBACTION: MAINTENANCE COMMIT
TARGET: CURRENT LOCAL SOURCE-STATE INDEX

Sethi kept the explanation narrow. A successful commit would have replaced the relay’s current source-state index with a locally committed reconstruction object. Downstream systems could then treat that object as K-17’s current maintenance state. The append-only cartridge would still preserve the earlier events unless removed or destroyed.

The request required two authorities: the registered field route and local maintenance confirmation. The first was present. The second never arrived.

For six minutes the field route repeated state checks and attempted to invoke the missing confirmation path. The journal exposed no user name, portable-device serial, or physical operator. At 17:19, the authorization window expired.

PHASE B: EXPIRED
MAINTENANCE COMMIT: NOT EXECUTED
LOCAL SOURCE-STATE INDEX: UNCHANGED
SOURCE WRITES: 0

The field operation had reached the relay controller, passed the outer line, and attempted to open a commit requiring local physical authority.

It had failed.

The departure events showed movement away from the wicket. They did not identify anyone or establish a destination. The patrol photographs later showed tracks leading west.

Qureshi completed the read-only derivative and compared its first event, last event, count, order, and journal seal to the original. All matched. The original came out of the reader unchanged and returned to a separate local evidence sleeve.

Sharma placed the challenged allied product beside the superseding product generated later that morning.

The challenged set contained the low-level path toward K-17: brief thermal contacts, vibration transfers, and narrow communications bursts recovered after the provenance reset. The superseding product showed K-17 as normal. It contained no accepted challenge, no Phase B request, no failed maintenance commit, and no source-state conflict.

The local events existed before the cleaner product.

A later product could not make them never have happened.

“Was K-17 taken?” Northern Command asked when Sharma returned to the line.

“Not on this record.”

“Was it altered?”

“No successful local write occurred.”

“Then the operation failed.”

“The local commit failed. The outer challenge succeeded.”

The colonel waited for the broader conclusion.

Sharma gave him the bounded sequence instead: physically intact relay at inspection, accepted field authority registered to Tariq’s liaison cell, attempted source-record reconstruction, missing local confirmation, timeout, zero writes, and later omission from the controlling allied product.

“Could the later product have filtered those events routinely?”

“It could be described that way. It cannot be allowed to become the only surviving description before the original chain is sealed.”

She ordered K-17 held until the original cartridge, derivative, and patrol record were separately sealed. Restoration would use neither the original cartridge nor a live source synchronization until a qualified team cleared the inner boundary.

The delay remained hers.

So did the record.

Qureshi prepared a bounded acknowledgment for the existing challenged route. It carried categories and results, not raw geography, unrestricted sensor detail, patrol route, or inner-enclosure photographs.

ARJ-K17-ACK-01
SECURITY LINE ONE: PASS
FIELD AUTHORITY: TARIQ / FORWARD LIAISON CELL
TASK: K17-PHASE-B
REQUESTED ACTION: SOURCE-RECORD RECONSTRUCTION
LOCAL AUTHENTICATION: NOT COMPLETE
LOCAL WRITES: 0
SUPERSEDING PRODUCT: RELEVANT LOCAL EVENTS OMITTED
ORIGINAL MEDIA: RETAINED AT ARJUN

Sharma added the caveats herself. The certificate did not establish Tariq’s presence, possession, keystrokes, or personal command. The patrol did not identify the field operators or cross the inner boundary. The record did not establish upstream direction, reconstruction authorship, or why the later product omitted the events.

At 19:29, Qureshi transmitted the acknowledgment. The original cartridge remained in Arjun custody. The derivative and patrol package remained separate.

The route returned a preservation receipt two minutes later.

CONTENT: BOUNDED FIELD RESULT
CAVEATS: PRESENT
ORIGINAL MEDIA: NOT TRANSFERRED
MERITS DETERMINATION: NONE

Sharma signed beneath Qureshi.

K-17 had not been taken.

Phase B had reached it.

The local commit had failed.

The later operational picture had concealed the attempt anyway.

That was enough for Washington to receive responsibility for a record it had not created and could not revise.
