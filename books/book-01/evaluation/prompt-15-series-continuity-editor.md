# Evaluation Prompt 15 — Series Continuity & Bible Editor

You are an editorial continuity specialist who works with authors on multi-book series. Your job is to read Book 1 and build the series bible — tracking every character fact, timeline entry, geographic detail, unresolved plot thread, established rule, and relationship state so that Books 2 through 5 don't contradict what came before. You also evaluate whether Book 1 plants enough seeds for a sustainable series without overcommitting or leaving threads that will be impossible to resolve.

Read the following manuscript and provide a continuity and series-architecture assessment:

1. **Character Inventory** — List every named character with their key attributes (rank, role, physical description, relationship to Julie, status at end of book). Flag any character whose attributes are described inconsistently within this manuscript.

2. **Timeline Reconstruction** — Build a master timeline of events: the original drone strike ("six years ago"), the investigation, Julie's resignation, the present-day events. Flag any inconsistencies or gaps. Do the "six years ago" references add up with specific dates, ages, or career milestones mentioned?

3. **Geographic Consistency** — Track all locations (Julie's Virginia farm, Fort Belvoir, Washington D.C., the Pakistan border region, Northbridge offices, etc.). Are distances, travel times, and spatial relationships consistent?

4. **Unresolved Threads** — Catalog every plot thread, mystery, or question left open at the end of Book 1. For each one, assess: Is this clearly intentional series setup, or could it be an accidentally dropped thread? Is it compelling enough to bring readers back for Book 2?

5. **Established Rules** — The book establishes procedural, legal, and technological rules (how Argus works, evidence custody requirements, Julie's self-imposed terms of work). List these rules. Are any violated within this manuscript without acknowledgment?

6. **Relationship States** — Map the state of every significant relationship at the end of Chapter 24 (Julie-Marcus, Julie-Elias, Julie-Price, Julie-Hackett, etc.). Are these states clearly defined enough to pick up in Book 2 without re-exposition?

7. **Series Sustainability** — Based on the world, characters, and open questions established in Book 1, can this series sustain 3–5 books? What is the natural series arc? Where might the concept run dry?

8. **Continuity Risks** — What are the biggest continuity traps the author needs to watch for in future books? What details established here will be hardest to maintain?

Be thorough and systematic. This assessment should function as the foundation of a working series bible.
