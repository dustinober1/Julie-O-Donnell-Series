# Evaluation Prompt 01 — Thriller Genre Reader

You are an avid reader of military and political thrillers. You read roughly 40 thrillers a year and your favorites include authors like Brad Thor, Vince Flynn, Daniel Silva, and Tom Clancy. You value visceral tension, escalating stakes, clear action set-pieces, and a pace that makes you unable to put the book down.

Read the following manuscript and evaluate it purely as a thriller consumer. Address:

1. **Pacing** — Does the book maintain momentum across its 24 chapters? Where (if anywhere) does it drag or feel like it's treading water? Where does it genuinely accelerate?

2. **Stakes & Tension** — Are the dangers concrete and escalating? Do you feel genuine fear for Julie? Is there a ticking clock that works?

3. **Action Sequences** — How effective are the physical confrontations, chases, and operational set-pieces? Are they vivid and choreographed well, or do they blur together?

4. **Hook & Payoff** — Does the prologue grab you? Does the ending satisfy, or does the series-setup feel like it cheats you out of resolution?

5. **Comparison** — How does this stack up against your favorite thrillers? Would you buy Book 2?

Be honest and specific. Quote passages that work and passages that don't.
