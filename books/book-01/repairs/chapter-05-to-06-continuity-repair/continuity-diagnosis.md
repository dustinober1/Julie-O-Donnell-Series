## Conflict summary

The accepted Chapter 5 and Chapter 6 cannot currently be read as consecutive events.

Chapter 5 ends at approximately 16:14 EDT / 01:44 IST with Julie, Marcus, and Elias at a circular production-vault entrance. Elias has invoked a director-certificate compromise path, supplied biometric confirmation, and caused the circular vault door to begin turning. The active deadline is presented as a 16:30 allied certification.

Chapter 6 opens at 04:52 EDT / 14:22 IST with the same three characters already running beneath hydraulic shutter L3-7. The aluminum case is ahead of them, Marcus is last with the tool bag, a security door is opening behind him, and the operative deadline is a 05:00 external commit. The scene is written as the next seconds of the same pursuit.

The conflict therefore includes four linked failures:

1. **Clock:** 16:14 does not flow into 04:52 without more than twelve unexplained hours.
2. **Deadline architecture:** 16:30 is treated as final in Chapters 1–5, while Chapters 6–8 require a distinct 05:00 executable release.
3. **Geography:** a circular vault entrance does not lead into the lower-tier shutter, cooling galleries, and rear enclave gate established by Chapters 6–7.
4. **Action and custody:** Chapter 5 does not place Elias, Julie, Marcus, the aluminum case, the tool bag, or the recovery team in the positions required by the Chapter 6 opening.

## Deadline interpretation

The least invasive supported distinction is:

- **16:30 EDT / 02:00 IST:** allied **source certification**. The United States formally validates the threat assessment and transmits it into the bilateral channel.
- **05:00 EDT / 14:30 IST:** executable **counter-battery support external commit**. The machine-readable support product, targeting basis, and release status become available for the Indian firing decision.

This preserves the institutional danger of the first clock while retaining the accepted tactical climax at the second clock. The first deadline makes the lie official. The second can make it lethal.

## Geography interpretation

The accepted Chapter 5 circular-vault sequence must be removed. The core access lift instead routes a hostile-credential incident session into a **lower-tier incident-transfer corridor**. That corridor contains hydraulic isolation shutter **L3-7**. Reporting APX-DIR-0019 as potentially compromised triggers local physical containment, causes L3-7 to descend, and preserves the separate cooling-gallery route to the enclave.

This mechanism does not perform Chapter 7's authentication. Elias does not select the enclave emergency-administrator identity, enter the rotating PIN, provide the bound live-finger biometric, or sign the enclave challenge until Chapter 7.
