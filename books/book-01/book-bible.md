## Reader Promise

A grounded military/intelligence techno-thriller where a former Army intelligence officer must prove that a trusted warning system is manufacturing certainty before that certainty helps trigger geopolitical escalation.

## Core Premise

Six years after Julie O'Donnell warned that Argus Beta was swallowing engineered data, a new enterprise ARGUS system begins classifying a real convoy attack near the Line of Control in a way that could justify dangerous escalation. Julie is pulled back into the system that broke her, but this time the harm is larger, faster, and protected by contractors, politicians, and an official record that prefers certainty.

## Prologue Wound

The prologue shows Julie at a forward operating base near the Pakistan border, identifying engineered neatness in Argus Beta under command pressure.

## Present-Day Case

ARGUS-ENT-4.6 / RoboWarning classifies the convoy attack as Pakistan-linked state sponsorship with escalating consequences.

## Core Mechanism

- Continuity premium.
- Vendor-enriched provenance.
- Normalization hiding self-corroboration.
- Whitelisted integration endpoint.
- Vendor relay/log purge.
- Confidence collapse after contaminated packets are isolated.

## Main Cast

## Ending Shape

The immediate crisis is resolved by restoring uncertainty and pausing action. The ending does not prove the system is safe and does not fully repair the official record.
