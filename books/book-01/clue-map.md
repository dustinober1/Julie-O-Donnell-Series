# Book 1 Clue Map

## Purpose

Track the fair-play clue progression for Book 1.

## Central Reveal

ARGUS-ENT-4.6 / RoboWarning is not independently corroborating the crisis classification. Its confidence is inflated by contaminated, vendor-enriched, dependent provenance routed through trusted channels.

## Clue Categories

### Data Clues

- Confidence rises faster than independent evidence should allow.
- Signals appear too clean or too synchronized.
- Removing certain packets should collapse confidence.

### Provenance Clues

- Multiple streams may share a hidden origin.
- Vendor-enriched metadata makes dependent sources appear independent.
- Whitelisted integration path bypasses normal suspicion.

### Human Clues

- Reed Calder has access and old ARGUS-family knowledge.
- Vance benefits from clean certainty and contained public messaging.
- Selene Cho understands the vulnerability but resists public admission.
- Hargrove's old command-pressure logic echoes in present choices.

### Institutional Clues

- Officials prefer a decisive classification.
- Oversight language protects systems more than truth.
- Julie's access is limited because uncertainty is dangerous to the action path.

## Clue Timing Rules

- Early chapters: show anomalous confidence and Julie's recognition.
- Middle chapters: reveal provenance dependency and access constraints.
- Late chapters: connect Reed Calder, vendor pathway, and old Argus Beta pattern.
- Final act: isolate contaminated packets and show confidence collapse.

## Do Not Reveal Too Early

- Exact whitelisted endpoint.
- Reed Calder's full role.
- Full old Argus Beta connection.
- The precise packet isolation test that collapses confidence.
