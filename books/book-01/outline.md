# Book 1 Outline - Complete Working Draft

## Status

Expanded commercial thriller outline for drafting preparation. This outline preserves existing canon and uses provisional but control-doc-integrated plot mechanics, supporting roles, and chapter turns for Book 1. Names and exact titles remain provisional unless locked in `PROJECT_STATE.yaml` or `docs/06_Continuity_Canon_Locks.md`.

## Working title

**Untitled Julie O'Donnell Book 1**

## Book promise

Julie O'Donnell, a former Army intelligence captain ruined after warning that an AI-enabled targeting system was swallowing engineered data, is pulled back toward a scaled-up ARGUS/Apex Defense system when its present-day enterprise warning output threatens to push a Pakistan/India crisis toward escalation.

## Target structure

- Target length: 100,000-125,000 words.
- Target chapters: Prologue plus 50 numbered chapters.
- Average chapter length: 2,000-2,400 words, with the prologue at 1,800-2,300 words and shorter high-pressure chapters in Act IV.
- Drafting budget: keep most chapters under 2,500 words; only Chapters 1, 23, 37, 46, and 49 should be allowed to run longer if the scene earns it.
- Primary POV: Julie O'Donnell.
- Cutaway POVs: contractor/government/political/antagonist only when they increase pressure or dramatic irony.
- Provisional present-day incident: an attack on a joint logistics convoy near the Line of Control produces ambiguous signals that ARGUS-ENT-4.6 / RoboWarning classifies as Pakistan-linked state sponsorship with Indian escalation implications.
- Provisional antagonist path: an intelligence-adjacent data broker and an Apex-aligned containment faction exploit a contaminated source-provenance path; political/command actors accelerate the decision because the model gives them cover.

## Key provisional roles for this outline

- **Mara Keene:** old colleague and current government access controller who brings Julie back under supervised conditions.
- **Evan Rusk:** former lead analyst from the prologue incident, now a cautious internal skeptic with guilt and access to buried technical history.
- **Vance:** political containment figure tied to oversight optics, appropriations pressure, and ARGUS reputation management. Exact title remains unresolved.
- **Dr. Selene Cho:** Apex Defense executive/technical face who defends ARGUS-ENT-4.6 with polished, plausible evasions.
- **Reed Calder:** data broker / former contractor liaison who exploits provenance assumptions and steers selected feeds into the warning chain.
- **Lieutenant Colonel Hargrove:** prologue command-pressure figure who later becomes an uneasy witness to the original Argus Beta failure.

### Prologue - The Pattern Was Too Clean

**Chapter number:** Prologue  
**Working title:** The Pattern Was Too Clean  
**POV:** Julie O'Donnell  
**Location:** Forward Operating Base near the Pakistan border, six years ago  
**Story function:** Establish Julie's competence, the original wound, Argus Beta's authority, and command pressure.  
**Conflict:** Julie sees that the SIGINT/timestamp pattern feeding Argus Beta is engineered, while Lieutenant Colonel Hargrove pushes action because the system confidence is high and a package is already in the air.
