# Book 1 Outline — Working Draft

## Status

Initial reverse-engineered outline scaffold. This is not yet a full chapter-by-chapter commercial outline.

## Working title

**Untitled Julie O'Donnell Book 1**

## Book promise

Julie O'Donnell, a former Army intelligence captain ruined after warning that an AI-enabled targeting system was swallowing engineered data, is pulled back toward a scaled-up ARGUS/Apex Defense system when its present-day enterprise warning output threatens to push a Pakistan/India crisis toward escalation.

## Target structure

- Target length: 85,000–100,000 words.
- Target chapters: 40–55.
- Primary POV: Julie O'Donnell.
- Cutaway POVs: contractor/government/political/antagonist only when they increase pressure or dramatic irony.

## Act I — The woman they did not believe

### Prologue — Six Years Ago: Forward Operating Base near the Pakistan border

**POV:** Julie O'Donnell  
**Function:** Establish wound, competence, system danger, and command pressure.  
**Core event:** Julie sees that the SIGINT/timestamp pattern feeding Argus Beta is too perfect and likely engineered. Hargrove pressures action because the system confidence is high and a package is in the air.  
**Hook:** Julie's warning is overridden or contained, setting up the career-breaking incident.

### Chapter 1 — The official version

**POV:** Julie O'Donnell  
**Function:** Show the institution creating the cover story that will define Julie's fall.  
**Location:** Intelligence oversight hearing, Senate/congressional closed session, or classified inquiry room.  
**Core event:** Julie is questioned about the prologue incident while officials, contractor-aligned experts, and politically protected advocates steer the record toward "officer hesitation" instead of Argus Beta failure.  
**Conflict:** Julie tries to explain engineered data and model vulnerability while the room pressures her to accept a cleaner narrative: the program worked, command acted, and her doubt introduced risk.  
**Hook:** Julie realizes the hearing is not designed to find the failure. It is designed to protect the program.

### Chapter 2 — Culpeper quiet

**POV:** Julie  
**Function:** Establish present-day Julie's civilian life, competence, isolation, and avoidance of government systems after the hearing/cover-story wound.  
**Location:** Culpeper, Virginia farmhouse/property.  
**Core event:** Julie handles a practical property problem while a trigger or message from the old world intrudes.  
**Hook:** Someone connected to the old system reaches out or an alert/news item echoes the prologue pattern.

### Chapter 3 — The ask she does not want

**POV:** Julie or government handler  
**Function:** Pull Julie toward the present crisis through the one person willing to believe her.  
**Core event:** An old colleague tells Julie that ARGUS-ENT-4.6 / RoboWarning is producing a high-confidence classification involving a Pakistan-linked incident and that the pattern resembles what she warned about six years earlier.  
**Conflict:** Julie refuses to be used as institutional cover, especially by people who once let the official record blame her hesitation instead of the program.  
**Hook:** The old colleague reveals that the new system is moving faster than the oversight chain can slow down.

### Chapter 4 — Containment language

**POV:** Vance / contractor / political cutaway  
**Function:** Show that Julie's involvement is being managed, not welcomed.  
**Core event:** Stakeholders discuss how to use Julie's expertise while limiting access and liability. The political layer is visible: ARGUS is tied to appropriations, donor networks, hearings, and officials who cannot afford a public failure.  
**Hook:** Julie will get only supervised sandbox access.

### Chapter 5 — Return to the machine

**POV:** Julie  
**Function:** Julie enters the controlled environment.  
**Core event:** She sees the ARGUS-ENT-4.6 output and immediately notices the confidence is too clean or too fast.  
**Hook:** The sandbox omits the one data path she needs.

## Act II — The sandbox

### Core act goal

Julie must determine whether the present-day warning is a real state-sponsored threat or an engineered attribution cascade while everyone around her wants a fast, actionable answer.

### Required beats

- Julie is restricted, monitored, or treated as unstable.
- ARGUS outputs are treated as authoritative by people under pressure.
- Apex defends the system without admitting uncertainty.
- Contradictory evidence exists but is delayed, downranked, or outside Julie's access.
- A human actor benefits from the current classification.
- Julie's prologue and hearing experience become relevant evidence, not just backstory.

## Act III — The buried pattern

### Core act goal

Julie moves from suspicion to proof while the institution moves from caution to action.

### Required beats

- Julie leaves or exceeds the sandbox.
- She reconstructs data lineage from partial access.
- An ally must decide whether to risk career or safety.
- The antagonist/containment faction accelerates the decision clock.
- Julie is publicly or officially discredited.
- The geopolitical situation worsens.
- Hargrove or another prologue witness may become relevant.

## Act IV — The decision no model can own

### Core act goal

Julie must force a human accountability moment before leaders act on a false model-shaped certainty.

### Required beats

- Escalation window narrows.
- Julie has evidence but lacks clean authority.
- The antagonist/containment faction frames delay as dangerous.
- A decision-maker must choose between model confidence and exposed uncertainty.
- Julie's prologue/hearing wound is mirrored and transformed.
- The immediate crisis is stopped or altered, but the larger system problem survives.
