# Book 1 AI Threat Design

## System

ARGUS-ENT-4.6 with RoboWarning warning-classification module.

## Domain

National-security warning classification with geopolitical escalation risk.

## Failure Mode

Trusted-source contamination and confidence inflation through provenance laundering and dependent corroboration.

## Mechanism Components

- Continuity premium.
- Vendor-enriched provenance.
- Normalization hiding self-corroboration.
- Whitelisted integration endpoint.
- Vendor relay/log purge.
- Confidence collapse after contaminated packets are isolated.

## Human Access Path

Reed Calder has a current vendor-provenance role and an old Argus Beta field-trial connection. Exact title remains unresolved.

## Institutional Protection

Apex Defense, political oversight, crisis decision-makers, and command-pressure culture all have reasons to prefer the system's clean confidence over Julie's uncertainty.

## Reader Explanation

The system is not hacked in a flashy way. It is fed information through channels it was already trained and authorized to trust.

## Reveal Ladder

1. RoboWarning produces dangerous certainty.
2. Julie notices confidence rising despite weak independence.
3. Provenance begins to look too clean.
4. Vendor-enriched packets appear independent but are dependent.
5. Whitelisted endpoint and relay behavior narrow the access path.
6. Contaminated packets are isolated and confidence collapses.
7. Human motive and old Argus Beta connection are exposed.
