## 19. Technology rules

1. **Identity field is not human authorship.** A displayed name, requestor field, office, badge, certificate, or inherited request reference identifies a system assertion, not the person who typed or approved the later action.
2. **Physical or independently authenticated act required.** Human attribution requires a matching physical signing event, local release record, authenticated session, witness act, or source-controlled custody event appropriate to the system.
3. **Request inheritance must be explicit.** A continuity broker may lawfully reference an earlier request, but the eventual prose must show which fields were inherited, which were added, when the derivation occurred, and what record owns that transformation.
4. **No generic hacking.** Production occurs through lawful custodians, source-limited derivatives, recorded interviews, and existing preservation authority.
5. **No magical access.** Julie cannot operate systems or retrieve records from restraint. Grant and source custodians perform every technical or production act.
6. **No signer operation.** `SSO-NS-004` remains closed and under no-use hold.
7. **No WSS decryption.** Existing encrypted blocks remain encrypted.
8. **No universal clock.** Office, DIA, LSS, WSS, Hartwell, MPD/DCIS, Apex, and Arjun clocks remain distinct. Any comparison records source clock, documented offset or tolerance, and uncertainty.
9. **No missing-event overclaim.** Absence of a Price physical event supports a bounded mismatch only after the relevant original and complete authorized event range are established.
10. **No employment-as-command inference.** Office role, DIA employment, Apex control, or Sterling-office registration cannot substitute for the missing human act.
11. **No automatic criminal meaning.** Identity reuse, inheritance, mirroring, or mapping may be unauthorized, malicious, administrative, or mixed. The record must establish mechanism before intent.
12. **No sealed-media shortcut.** Existing MPD findings may be referenced from accepted reports; no package is reopened.
