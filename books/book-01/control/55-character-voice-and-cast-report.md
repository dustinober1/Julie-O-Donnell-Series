# Book 1 Character Voice and Cast Report

**Current state:** Voice architecture defined; dialogue pass begins only after technical corrections and late-act compression stabilize the factual language.

## Purpose

The manuscript's institutions share a need for precise proof language. That shared rigor must not make Julie, Marcus, Elias, Sarah, Mercer, Ortiz, Grant, Alvarez, and Sharma sound interchangeable.

## Voice controls

### Julie O'Donnell

- Default lens: physical cause, observed contradiction, proof ceiling.
- Sentence behavior: direct, sparse, often turns an overclaim into a narrower fact.
- Emotional behavior: avoids self-explanation; emotion enters through attention, bodily memory, and decisions.
- Avoid: making her explain every evidentiary principle after another character has demonstrated it.
- Signature questions: `What does it prove?` `Who owns the source?` `What changed physically?`

### Marcus Reed

- Default lens: command responsibility, access, route, consequence, who must act.
- Sentence behavior: operational summaries, testing the plan's limits, concise admissions when he finally stops defending himself.
- Emotional behavior: regret expressed through custody and action rather than abstract theory.
- Avoid: giving him Julie's exact proof-ceiling vocabulary in every scene.
- Signature questions: `Who receives it?` `What is the abort?` `Can we move?`

### Elias Thorne

- Default lens: mechanism, system state, identity ownership, consent, what an interface actually does.
- Sentence behavior: qualifying clauses and accelerated technical explanation under fear; sharper boundaries after custody.
- Emotional behavior: tremor, speed, anger at others assigning his choices.
- Avoid: turning him into a calm omniscient explainer or automatic future team member.
- Signature questions: `Which identity domain?` `What state sequence?` `Who owns my act?`

### Sarah Chen

- Default lens: categories, source order, reviewability, defensible command language.
- Sentence behavior: formal and controlled; becomes sharper when an order asks procedure to conceal a material change.
- Emotional behavior: restraint and exact source certification, not confession or alliance.
- Avoid: giving her Julie's moral rhetoric or later knowledge she did not possess.
- Signature actions: preserve both directives; certify category and order; refuse to characterize motive.

### Daniel Mercer

- Default lens: physical containment, force geometry, receiving authority, orders he can defend later.
- Sentence behavior: commands, distances, backgrounds, thresholds.
- Emotional behavior: uncertainty appears as requests for named custody or force clarification.
- Avoid: extended technical argument.
- Signature questions: `Confirm force standard.` `Who receives custody?`

### Officer Gabriel Ortiz

- Default lens: immediate public scene, visible hands, item condition, body-camera record, safe movement.
- Sentence behavior: plain procedural language; impatient with intelligence theory but willing to route a precise preservation request.
- Emotional behavior: professional caution, not sudden belief in Julie.
- Avoid: source-integrity jargon unless he repeats a subject statement with attribution.
- Signature questions: `What can I observe?` `Who takes this from me?`

### MPD Watch Commander Helena Brooks

- Default lens: make the scene survivable, separate public safety from private recovery claims.
- Sentence behavior: decisive command and accountability.
- Avoid: technical findings or conspiracy conclusions.

### Leila Grant

- Default lens: smallest testable question, independent method, repeatability, direct observation.
- Sentence behavior: names what a source can answer and forces the proposed alternate mechanism to produce its own evidence.
- Emotional behavior: intellectual discipline, not admiration for Julie.
- Avoid: adopting Julie's theory before independent sources converge.
- Signature questions: `What direct question can this object answer?` `What would falsify it?`

### Miriam Alvarez

- Default lens: named responsibility, jurisdiction, source routing, scope, who owns delay and loss.
- Sentence behavior: assigns authority and preserves objections rather than debating every technical detail.
- Avoid: becoming the technical examiner or benevolent fixer.
- Signature questions: `Who accepts the incident?` `What remains outside our authority?`

### Major Ananya Sharma

- Default lens: terrain, soldiers, local command, the cost of converting likely into finished.
- Sentence behavior: concrete operational distinctions; refuses headquarters language that exceeds local observation.
- Emotional behavior: responsibility grounded in remembered human consequence.
- Avoid: Washington-style evidence-law phrasing.
- Signature questions: `What did the patrol see?` `Who owns the firing decision?`

### Arthur Vance

- Default lens: continuity, institutional defensibility, reduced ambiguity, program survival.
- Sentence behavior: polished and strategically incomplete; treats uncertainty as a failure of governance.
- Avoid: full villain confession, omniscient exposition, or language that proves the original constructor.

## High-risk interchangeable constructions

Review every occurrence of the following after technical editing:

- `That wasn't the question.`
- `It was not the same fact.`
- `The record did not prove...`
- `Registered authority is not physical possession.`
- `Preservation is not production.`
- `Not established.`
- `Name the receiving authority.`

Retain them when they are character-defining or legally necessary. Elsewhere, shift the same limit into the speaker's native lens.

## Cast orientation

### Essential distinct roles

Do not merge:

- Ortiz and Brooks — scene officer versus watch command.
- Grant and Alvarez — technical method versus federal receiving responsibility.
- Vega and Vann — legislative authority versus physical compromise-control custodian.
- Rao and Sethi — patrol commander/witness versus local relay custodian.
- Bell and the Northbridge operator — sponsoring facilities authority versus session operator.
- Sarah and Mercer — compliance/source order versus physical containment.

### Role reminders required on return

When a secondary character returns after a chapter gap, the first sentence or action should restore function without a biography:

- `MPD Officer Gabriel Ortiz, who had controlled the garage handoff...`
- `Special Agent Leila Grant, the independent technical examiner...`
- `Miriam Alvarez, the DCIS official who had accepted federal responsibility...`
- `Marisol Vega, the Legislative Secure Services deputy authority...`
- `Martin Vann, the named compromise-control custodian...`
- `Diane Kessler, Sterling's deputy national-security director and the recorded exception authorizer...`
- `Samuel Drennan, the office courier shown carrying the closed signer case...`
- `Captain Arvind Rao, the K-17 patrol commander...`
- `Naib Subedar Vikram Sethi, K-17's local custodian...`

The exact wording should vary with POV and scene rhythm.

## Consolidation review

A minor official may be merged only when all of the following are true:

1. The character appears once.
2. The character performs no independent source-custody, authority, consent, or witness function.
3. Removing the name does not merge two institutional roles.
4. The dialogue can be reassigned without giving another character knowledge or authority they should not possess.

Potential candidates should be identified during copyedit, not preselected before the legal and authority review.

## Dialogue pass method

1. Extract dialogue by chapter.
2. Remove tags from a working copy.
3. Mark lines that could be spoken unchanged by three or more principals.
4. Preserve meaning and facts while rewriting only those lines through the speaker's lens.
5. Recheck that proof ceilings remain exact.
6. Read each principal's dialogue continuously across the novel.
7. Update this report with examples changed and examples deliberately retained.

## Completion gate

- Character voices remain distinguishable without relying solely on dialogue tags.
- No character gains later knowledge early.
- Shared evidentiary rigor remains, but its expression differs by role.
- Late characters are reoriented on return without excessive exposition.
- Elias's boundaries, Marcus's incomplete repair, Sarah's institutional position, and Sharma's local command voice remain intact.
