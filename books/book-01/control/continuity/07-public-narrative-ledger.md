## Public-narrative continuity rules

1. Every official statement must reflect what its issuer knew and owned at that moment.
2. Media may simplify, but the manuscript must preserve the difference between headline and authenticated source.
3. A later correction does not erase the original alert, accusation, or harm.
4. The final correction fixes causal authorship without declaring global innocence.
5. Do not let the narrator adopt a public overclaim merely because a headline does.
6. Preserve the aborted overclaim as evidence that even correction requires source limits.
