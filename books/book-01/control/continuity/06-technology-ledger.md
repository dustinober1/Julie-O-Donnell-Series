## Technology-continuity rules

1. No system is omniscient; every event has a local source, clock, and proof ceiling.
2. `Immutable` may be used only if the specialist confirms the mechanism; otherwise prefer narrower language.
3. A certificate or registered authority does not prove the human hand.
4. A hardware response does not prove message understanding or command.
5. Hashes prove byte continuity within scope, not truth or completeness.
6. A missing physical-token event may exclude that token without identifying the alternate mechanism.
7. A derivative never silently replaces the source original.
8. Any correction to a device's capability must be propagated through every chapter that relies on it.
