## Continuity rules

1. Injuries accumulate; no chapter resets pain or capability.
2. Adrenaline may explain brief action, not sustained normal performance.
3. A successful action does not erase later medical consequence.
4. Medical authority can end writing, questioning, standing, or custody without weakening the evidence chain.
5. Injury never functions as automatic redemption, consent, or moral proof.
6. Any specialist correction must identify every downstream action whose feasibility changes.
