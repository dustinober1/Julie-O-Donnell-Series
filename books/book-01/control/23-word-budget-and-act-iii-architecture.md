## Expansion and compression controls

### Expand when

- the physical or legal meaning of MPD scene control is unclear;
- a crucial evidence distinction is not dramatized;
- the Hartwell held record is treated as self-authenticating instead of requiring lawful examination;
- K-17 and Washington are not causally reconnected;
- Phase B remains only a deadline;
- a recurring character’s consequential choice lacks setup;
- Marcus’s corrective action lacks cost; or
- Julie’s ending role is asserted rather than chosen.

### Compress when

- characters repeat Chapter 14’s body-camera statement or evidence inventory;
- another chase reproduces an earlier escape without a new consequence;
- technical explanation does not alter a decision;
- Ortiz, Mercer, or another officer becomes a transcript-delivery device rather than a pressure-bearing actor;
- an antagonist scene says only what the held records already show; or
- a series seed competes with Book 1 closure.

## Final length review

At the end of the first full draft:

1. Recount accepted words only.
2. Audit each chapter’s dominant function.
3. Cut repeated explanation before cutting consequence.
4. Add missing decision or aftermath scenes before adding procedural detail.
5. Keep the final manuscript within 100,000–125,000 words.
