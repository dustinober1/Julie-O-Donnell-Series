## Ranks and titles

- Capitalize a rank when used directly before a name: `Major Sharma`, `Colonel Reed`.

## Numbers and percentages

- Spell out simple narrative numbers when natural; use numerals in displays, timestamps, serials, measurements, percentages, and evidence counts.
- `94.1 percent`, `99.8 percent`, `0.07 percent`, `8.4 percent` in prose unless a system display uses `%`.
- Measurements may use numerals in technical displays and words in narrative according to rhythm.
- Keep `forty-three-second delay` hyphenated as a compound modifier.

## System displays

- Use plain uppercase lines, not Markdown backticks.
- Leave a blank line before and after a display block when prose readability requires it.
- Do not add quotation marks around system text.
- Preserve exact identifier spelling and status order when continuity depends on it.
- Avoid excessive display repetition when a later scene can summarize an unchanged state.

## Scene headings

- Chapter headings: `# Chapter N — Title`.
- Prologue heading: retain accepted form and manifest title.
- Scene headings use location and time on separate lines as established in the manuscript.
- No leading spaces on scene-heading lines.
- No chapter heading attached to preceding prose.

## Dialogue and voice

- Preserve dialogue meaning.
- Maintain speaker-specific reasoning described in `55-character-voice-and-cast-report.md`.
- Use contractions according to established character voice; do not mechanically expand or contract all dialogue.
- Avoid assigning Julie's `What does it prove?` rhythm to every professional character.

## Proof language

Keep these distinctions exact:

- `registered to` does not mean physically held by.
- `physical carrier` does not mean authorizer.
- `authorizer` does not mean instruction source.
- `hardware response` does not mean human understanding or command.
- `physical token did not sign` does not identify the alternate operator.
- `not established` does not mean disproved.
- `preserved` does not mean produced or admitted.
- `received` does not mean adopted as true.
- `hash match` does not prove source truth or completeness.

## Repetition watch list

Review but do not automatically delete:

- `That wasn't the question.`
- `That is not the same fact.`
- `The record did not prove...`
- `Not established.`
- `No one celebrated.`
- `He/She did not answer.`
- repeated full seven-package inventory.
- repeated `136 / 47 / 311` counts.
- repeated camera, hands-visible, and clock-source setup.
- `Not X. Not Y. Z.` paragraph rhythm.

## AI-rhythm controls

- Preserve short paragraphs for countdowns, reversals, physical danger, and emotionally consequential decisions.
- Integrate routine reactions, explanations, and transitions into fuller paragraphs where isolation adds no pressure.
- Avoid repeated three-beat fragments with identical syntax.
- Avoid excessive abstract restatement after a scene has already demonstrated the point.
- Do not replace spare prose with decorative metaphor.

## Spelling and punctuation

- American English.
- Smart apostrophes/quotation marks in production output; source Markdown may retain UTF-8 smart punctuation already present.
- Em dash without surrounding spaces in prose.
- Ellipses only for actual trailing or interrupted speech; prefer em dash for interruption.
- Serial and code punctuation is literal and not typographically normalized.

## Content locks for copyedit

Copyedit may not change:

- plot or outcome;
- chapter/scene/POV order;
- character decisions or knowledge;
- evidence contents or custody;
- technology capability or proof ceiling;
- institutional authority;
- dialogue meaning;
- open series threads;
- final line.

Any query that may affect one of these goes back to the specialist findings ledger and continuity ledgers before acceptance.
