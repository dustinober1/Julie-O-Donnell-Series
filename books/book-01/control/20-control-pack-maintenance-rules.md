# 20. CONTROL-PACK MAINTENANCE RULES

## Authority and placement

1. `../ACCEPTED_MANUSCRIPT.yaml` controls accepted prose.
2. Accepted prose lives under `../manuscript/`; unaccepted prose under `../drafts/`.
3. `../../../PROJECT_STATE.yaml` controls production navigation, not canon.
4. `../../../series/recurring-character-ledger.md` controls cross-book tracking where it agrees with accepted prose.
5. Archive files and external snapshots are historical provenance only.

## Promotion and acceptance

6. Every chapter begins with a mission lock or approved brief.
7. A draft remains non-canon until a formal acceptance gate issues one explicit verdict.
8. An **ACCEPT** promotion synchronizes prose, manifest, totals, endpoint, state, status, materially affected ledgers, review record, and permanent validation.
9. Do not leave active final/latest/backup/alternate duplicates; Git history preserves superseded prose.
10. Review-authorized prose repairs must be minimal, documented, recounted, and rehashed before promotion.

## Continuity, evidence, knowledge, and publication

11. Record EDT and IST together when cross-border state is affected.
12. Update injuries only after accepted treatment and elapsed time.
13. Keep originals and derivatives separate; record source, range, clock, method, integrity, movement, authority, withheld fields, and proof limit.
14. Request identity, construction mechanism, authority binding, release act, registered authority, physical act, custody, carrier assignment, authorization, knowledge, intent, direction, and command are separate proof categories.
15. A certified derivative or public instrument never replaces an original.
16. A no-use hold preserves state/custody and does not authenticate prior operation.
17. Never invent incomplete, excluded, encrypted, unavailable, privileged, destroyed, or unexamined contents.
18. Public hashes/receipts protect the publication object, not every underlying source.
19. Update knowledge, thread, ending, public/private, and recurring-character controls after every accepted chapter.

## Plot, word count, and revision

20. Resolve Book 1 obligations before using a series hook to defer them.
21. Future-book roles require accepted Book 1 state.
22. Unwritten chapters become controlling only through individual mission locks.
23. Do not create a complete chapter-by-chapter remainder-of-Act-III outline.
24. Recount accepted prose after every promotion; keep Book 1 within 100,000–125,000 words.
25. Narrative House Style v2.2 controls craft, not canon.
26. No prose may imitate or closely emulate the recognizable style of a living author.
