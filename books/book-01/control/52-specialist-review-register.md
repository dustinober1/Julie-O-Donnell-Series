## Minimum useful reviewer profiles

- **SIGINT / ELINT / provenance:** Signals-intelligence, electronic-warfare, telemetry, sensor-fusion, collection, or source-provenance practitioner.
- **Military targeting / UAS / JAG:** Targeting officer, MQ-9 aircrew/operations specialist, operational-law attorney, or military intelligence professional with strike-chain experience.
- **Secure facility / fire / suppression:** Fire-protection engineer, clean-agent designer, classified-facility security professional, or code-enforcement specialist.
- **PKI / hardware token / forensics:** Applied cryptographer, HSM/token engineer, PKI architect, digital-forensics examiner, or secure-identity specialist.
- **Federal investigation / custody / legal:** Federal criminal practitioner, D.C. prosecutor or defense attorney, federal agent, evidence-custody specialist, or former MPD/federal liaison.
- **Indian Army / South Asia:** Indian Army officer, artillery practitioner, South Asia defense analyst, or Line-of-Control security specialist.
- **Trauma medicine:** Emergency physician, trauma surgeon, paramedic, sports-medicine physician, or clinician experienced with concussion, rib/chest, wrist/thumb, and hip injuries.

## Status values

- **UNREVIEWED:** No named qualification-backed deliverable exists.
- **IN REVIEW:** A real named reviewer has accepted the packet but has not delivered findings.
- **FINDINGS RECEIVED:** Deliverable exists; editorial adjudication is incomplete.
- **CORRECTIONS REQUIRED:** One or more accepted critical or material findings remain open.
- **APPROVED WITH CORRECTIONS:** Reviewer approved the mechanism after specified corrections; corrections still require continuity validation.
- **APPROVED:** Review is complete and no unresolved critical or material finding remains.
- **NOT APPROVED:** A central scene or mechanism remains implausible after attempted correction.

Packet readiness is an editorial-operations state only. It does not change an external status. All seven rows remain **UNREVIEWED**.

## Required evidence for closure

Each closed issue must contain or link to:

1. Reviewer name.
2. Relevant qualifications or experience.
3. Review date.
4. Exact manuscript version or accepted-manifest commit reviewed.
5. Findings ranked critical, material, minor, or preference.
6. Exact chapter and passage for every finding.
7. Smallest story-preserving correction.
8. Confirmation of credible material that should remain unchanged.
9. Final disposition.
10. Editorial response and the commit or PR containing accepted corrections.
11. Post-correction continuity validation.

## Editorial decision ceiling

- A reviewer may establish field credibility or identify errors.
- A reviewer does not control plot, theme, character arcs, or prose voice.
- The editor chooses the smallest correction that satisfies the factual issue while preserving the content lock.
- Conflicting specialist findings must remain open until reconciled by a reviewer with overlapping competence or by separate source-backed limits.
- No review can establish the original 02:14 human constructor or Sterling's personal command unless the manuscript itself is deliberately revised to reveal them. Those remain series carryovers.

## Publication gate

Publication remains blocked until all seven rows are **APPROVED** or **APPROVED WITH CORRECTIONS** with every accepted correction applied and continuity-validated. Packet synchronization does not mark the book ready for publication.
