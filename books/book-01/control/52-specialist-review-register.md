## Status definitions

- **UNREVIEWED:** No named qualification-backed deliverable exists.
- **IN REVIEW:** A named reviewer has accepted the packet but has not delivered findings.
- **FINDINGS RECEIVED:** A written deliverable exists; editorial adjudication is incomplete.
- **APPROVED WITH CORRECTIONS:** A named reviewer approved the mechanism after specified corrections.
- **APPROVED:** A named reviewer supplied an explicit approval with no unresolved critical or material finding.
- **NOT APPROVED:** A named reviewer found that a central mechanism remains implausible after attempted correction.
- **AUTHOR WAIVER:** The author elects to proceed without the formal documentation package and accepts the residual risk. This is not an external-review status and must not be described as approval.

## Evidence that would still be required to upgrade a waived track

A later qualification-backed closure must contain or link to:

1. reviewer name;
2. relevant qualifications or experience;
3. review date;
4. exact manuscript version or accepted-manifest commit reviewed;
5. findings ranked critical, material, minor, or preference;
6. exact chapter and passage for every finding;
7. smallest story-preserving correction;
8. confirmation of credible material that should remain unchanged;
9. final disposition;
10. editorial response and the commit or PR containing accepted corrections;
11. post-correction continuity validation.

## Editorial decision ceiling

- A reviewer may establish field credibility or identify errors.
- A reviewer does not control plot, theme, character arcs, or prose voice.
- The editor chooses the smallest correction that satisfies the factual issue while preserving the content lock.
- Conflicting specialist findings remain open until reconciled.
- No review can establish the original 02:14 human constructor or Sterling's personal command unless the manuscript is deliberately revised to reveal them. Those remain series carryovers.
