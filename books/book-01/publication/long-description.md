## Draft Direction

Former Army Captain Julie O'Donnell built her career on seeing patterns others missed. Six years ago, that skill ended her career when she warned that an AI-enabled targeting system was being fed engineered certainty. Command overrode her. The official record survived. Julie did not.

Now a new ARGUS enterprise warning system is classifying a border attack with dangerous confidence, and decision-makers are preparing to act on what the machine says it knows. Pulled back under supervision by an old colleague, Julie sees the same impossible neatness in a new interface.

The system is not alive. It is not evil. But someone understands what it trusts — and how to turn uncertainty into permission.

To stop a crisis before it becomes a war, Julie has to prove the one thing institutions are built to resist: the official record is wrong.
