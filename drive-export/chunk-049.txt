“Does physical administrator-token board EAT-0881147 contain a secure-element signing event, monotonic-counter transition, or local release record corresponding to the original deployment event attributed to Elias Thorne’s physical token?”

Nothing about innocence. Nothing about Payload 88. Nothing about Sterling, Vance, Tariq, K-17, Phase B, or the three encrypted WSS blocks.

The question was narrower than Julie had wanted when she first saw the board’s history in the cutout. It was also the first question someone outside the trio and Apex had agreed to test.

Julie felt the last phrase settle where hope had been trying to become certainty.

Ordinary materials doing honest work.

Apex counsel called before Grant arrived.

Separating those electronics from the paper and vehicle material was not fragmentation. It was technical necessity.

Ortiz listened without interrupting.

Julie watched his face rather than the speaker. The argument gave him lawful concerns: classification, contamination, incorrect tooling, an examiner misunderstanding a device built for a specialized environment. Apex did not need every official to be compromised. It needed the legitimate risks to point toward Apex control.
