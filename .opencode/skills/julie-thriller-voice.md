# Skill: Julie Thriller Voice

## Purpose

Maintain a commercial, grounded, high-tension thriller voice for the Julie O'Donnell series.

Use this skill when drafting, revising, line-editing, opening chapters, ending chapters, tightening dialogue, or removing AI-ish prose tells.

## Voice Target

Clean, tense, cinematic, readable prose. Procedural enough to feel credible, emotional enough to matter, never so technical that the story turns into a white paper.

## Style Pillars

- Immediate pressure.
- Concrete sensory detail.
- Tactical observation.
- Institutional menace.
- Human cost before abstract theory.
- Short-to-medium paragraphs during tension.
- Longer, controlled paragraphs only for analysis or moral pressure.

## Julie POV Rules

When in Julie's perspective:

- Show her reading systems and people together.
- Let her notice what is too neat, too smooth, too convenient, or missing.
- Let her distrust confidence without rejecting evidence.
- Let emotion leak through action, pressure, silence, and choices rather than direct labels.
- Her competence should be visible through prioritization, not exposition.

## Dialogue Rules

Dialogue should:

- carry pressure;
- reveal power dynamics;
- avoid exposition dumps;
- allow subtext;
- avoid every character sounding like a policy memo;
- preserve institutional evasiveness where appropriate.

Bad default line:

> The model appears to have been compromised through vendor-supplied data contamination.

Better thriller line:

> "It didn't break in," Julie said. "It was invited."

## Technical Exposition Rules

Technical explanation should be staged:

1. first as a strange effect;
2. then as a pattern;
3. then as a narrowed mechanism;
4. finally as motive plus exploit.

Never explain the entire system before the reader has a reason to care.

## Chapter Opening Rules

Open with one of:

- an immediate operational problem;
- a human consequence;
- a contradiction Julie cannot ignore;
- an institutional lie being formalized;
- a quiet image loaded with dread.

Avoid opening with abstract backstory, generic waking up, or direct explanation of AI concepts.

## Chapter Ending Rules

End with:

- a sharper question;
- a reversal;
- a human cost;
- a new constraint;
- a revealed omission;
- an institutional escalation.

Avoid fake cliffhangers that do not alter the investigation.

## AI-Ish Prose Tells to Remove

Watch for:

- repetitive sentence templates;
- overuse of "not X, but Y" constructions;
- generic emotional labels;
- everyone speaking in polished complete paragraphs;
- obvious theme statements;
- repeated phrases such as "the weight of," "a chill ran," "the silence stretched," unless specifically earned;
- summaries of what the scene already showed.

## Preferred Texture

Use precise verbs, concrete settings, and operational pressure.

The series should feel like contemporary government rooms, contractor dashboards, secure facilities, farmhouse isolation, hearing rooms, crisis cells, and the moral chill of systems that produce confident harm.
