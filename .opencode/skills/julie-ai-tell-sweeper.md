# Skill: Julie AI-Tell Sweeper

## Purpose

Identify and remove prose patterns that make the manuscript feel generated, generic, repetitive, or emotionally over-labeled.

Use this skill after drafting and line editing.

## Primary Risk Areas

- Repetitive sentence structure.
- Abstract emotional summaries.
- Generic thriller phrasing.
- Overly symmetrical paragraphs.
- Dialogue that sounds too polished.
- Exposition that explains theme instead of dramatizing pressure.
- Repeated gestures and beats.
- Recycled metaphors or images.
- Predictable chapter-end phrasing.

## Julie-Specific Watchlist

Watch for overuse of:

- Julie clenching her jaw;
- silence stretching;
- weight pressing on her chest;
- screens glowing ominously;
- systems described as monsters;
- institutions described only in abstract terms;
- characters "knowing" things without evidence;
- repeated references to being right too late without new context.

## Sweep Method

For each chapter:

1. Scan for repeated words, beats, and sentence patterns.
2. Flag repeated emotional labels.
3. Flag dialogue that sounds generated or over-expository.
4. Replace weak abstractions with specific pressure, image, or choice.
5. Preserve meaning and clue timing.
6. Do not add new plot.

## Output Requirements

Produce:

- revised chapter if edits are requested;
- AI-tell sweep report if audit-only;
- list of repeated phrases removed;
- any phrases intentionally preserved because they are motif or canon.

## Do Not Overcorrect

Do not remove all style consistency. A series needs recognizable voice. The goal is to remove mechanical repetition, not make every sentence maximally unusual.
