# Skill: Julie Prose Drafter

## Purpose

Draft chapter prose for the Julie O'Donnell series from approved blueprints while preserving canon, clue logic, tone, and commercial thriller pacing.

## Required Inputs

Before drafting, read:

- `.opencode/skills/julie-series-canon.md`
- `.opencode/skills/julie-thriller-voice.md`
- `.opencode/skills/julie-ai-plausibility.md`
- active book `book-bible.md`
- active chapter blueprint
- previous chapter prose, if available
- active book `clue-map.md`
- active book `continuity-log.md`

## Drafting Rules

- Draft only the requested chapter.
- Preserve all locked canon.
- Do not invent major plot turns beyond the blueprint.
- Do not resolve mystery questions earlier than the clue map allows.
- Do not reveal technical mechanism all at once.
- Do not make Julie omniscient.
- Do not make supporting characters explain the plot in speeches.
- Keep scenes grounded in physical space, pressure, and consequence.

## Prose Standard

The chapter should read like a polished commercial thriller draft, not an outline or summary.

Include:

- scene-level conflict;
- concrete setting details;
- dialogue with subtext;
- tactical observation;
- institutional pressure;
- emotional restraint;
- forward momentum.

Avoid:

- generic filler;
- repeated internal monologue;
- AI-ish sentence patterns;
- over-explaining every technical point;
- sudden melodrama;
- chapter endings that only promise future action without changing the present situation.

## Word Count Discipline

Respect the target range from the blueprint. If no target exists, use the book-level target average.

For Book 1 default:

- standard chapter: 2,100-2,400 words;
- tentpole chapter: 2,700-3,000 words;
- prologue may be shorter and sharper if needed.

## Output Requirements

Write the chapter in manuscript-ready Markdown:

```markdown
# Chapter X: Title

[prose]
```

Do not include revision notes inside the chapter file. Put notes in `revision-notes/` or the command report.
