# Skill: Julie Chapter Architect

## Purpose

Plan Julie O'Donnell chapters that move plot, clue logic, character pressure, and series continuity forward without drifting from canon.

Use this skill for chapter blueprints, scene lists, chapter repair, act structure, clue staging, and chapter-to-chapter continuity.

## Required Inputs

Before planning a chapter, read:

- `PROJECT_STATE.yaml`
- `.opencode/skills/julie-series-canon.md`
- `.opencode/skills/julie-thriller-voice.md`
- `.opencode/skills/julie-ai-plausibility.md`
- `series-bible/00-series-dashboard.md`
- `series-bible/09-continuity-locks.md`
- active book's `book-bible.md`
- active book's `chapter-outline.md`
- active book's `clue-map.md`
- active book's `mystery-engine.md`
- previous chapter, if it exists
- next chapter outline, if it exists

## Blueprint Standard

Each chapter blueprint should include:

- chapter number and working title;
- POV;
- target word count;
- purpose in the book;
- opening image or pressure;
- scene-by-scene sequence;
- clue movement;
- emotional movement;
- technical movement;
- antagonist/institutional pressure;
- continuity locks;
- ending hook;
- forbidden moves.

## Scene Design Rules

Every scene should do at least two of these:

- advance the investigation;
- reveal character under pressure;
- complicate the technical mechanism;
- increase institutional opposition;
- sharpen the moral stakes;
- expose contradiction between official certainty and real uncertainty;
- force Julie to choose between safety and truth.

## Mystery Movement Rules

A chapter should not merely restate the same suspicion. It must move the mystery through one of:

- new data;
- new contradiction;
- new witness;
- new institutional reaction;
- new motive pressure;
- new technical narrowing;
- new personal cost;
- new false lead that teaches something true.

## Julie Arc Rules

Do not heal Julie too quickly. Each chapter may move her through one specific pressure point:

- avoidance;
- suspicion;
- anger;
- reluctant engagement;
- tactical clarity;
- emotional cost;
- trust risk;
- confrontation with old record;
- acceptance that truth requires a public record.

## Output Format

Use `templates/chapter-blueprint-template.md` when available. Keep the blueprint specific enough that a drafting command can produce a chapter without inventing major plot or canon.
