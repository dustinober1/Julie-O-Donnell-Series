# Skill: Julie Line Editor

## Purpose

Revise Julie O'Donnell prose for tension, clarity, rhythm, emotional restraint, and commercial thriller quality without changing canon or story logic.

## Editing Priorities

1. Preserve canon and clue logic.
2. Strengthen scene pressure.
3. Remove summary where dramatization is needed.
4. Cut repetition and filler.
5. Sharpen dialogue.
6. Replace generic emotion with behavior, image, or choice.
7. Preserve Julie's tactical intelligence and emotional guardedness.
8. Maintain technical clarity without lecture.

## What to Improve

- Weak openings.
- Soft scene turns.
- Redundant exposition.
- Flat action beats.
- Generic body language.
- Overused thriller phrases.
- Dialogue that states what both characters already know.
- Paragraphs that explain instead of create pressure.

## What Not to Do

- Do not add new canon without a canon-update pass.
- Do not alter clue timing without checking the clue map.
- Do not make the villain more obvious for the sake of punch.
- Do not change technical mechanism to make prose easier.
- Do not overwrite Julie's voice with melodrama.
- Do not compress away important procedural steps that build plausibility.

## AI-Tell Sweep Checklist

Remove or reduce:

- repeated clause structures;
- repetitive internal labels;
- vague dread language;
- "as if" similes stacked too closely;
- excessive rhetorical triads;
- generic phrases such as "the weight of the moment";
- scene conclusions that restate the theme.

## Output Requirements

When editing a chapter, provide:

- revised chapter file only if editing is requested;
- revision report in `revision-notes/`;
- brief summary of major changes;
- list of any continuity or canon risks discovered but not changed.
