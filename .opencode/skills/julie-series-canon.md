# Skill: Julie Series Canon

## Purpose

Preserve durable continuity, character integrity, technical realism, and long-series coherence for the Julie O'Donnell thriller series.

This is the master canon skill. Treat it as the series constitution, not a scratchpad.

## Canon Update Rule

Chapter commands may discover canon. Only canon-maintenance commands may promote canon.

This skill may be changed only by an explicit canon-maintenance pass such as:

```text
/julie-canon-update <book-number> <chapter-number-or-final>
/julie-series-drift-audit <book-number-or-series>
```

## Core Series Identity

Julie O'Donnell is a former U.S. Army Captain and Army intelligence officer whose career was destroyed after she recognized that an AI-enabled SIGINT/targeting system was being fed engineered certainty under command pressure.

The series is a grounded contemporary intelligence / political / military techno-thriller series about systems that classify, escalate, optimize, and harm people when human incentives hide inside automated authority.

## Reader Promise

Every book should deliver:

- A grounded AI, algorithmic, data, cyber, military, institutional, or automated decision threat.
- A human victim or moral cost at the center.
- An investigation where clues hide in outputs, provenance, incentives, behavior, logs, omissions, and institutional pressure.
- A plausible technical mechanism readable by nontechnical thriller readers.
- A human villain, human exploiter, or institutionally protected motive behind the algorithmic harm.
- A Julie arc that deepens her trauma, competence, relationships, and unresolved Argus Beta reckoning.

## Julie O'Donnell Character Lock

Julie is:

- competent before she is wounded;
- tactical without becoming superhuman;
- morally stubborn under pressure;
- skeptical of algorithmic certainty, not reflexively anti-technology;
- emotionally guarded but not flat;
- capable of anger when institutions protect systems over people;
- haunted by being right too late;
- reluctant to return to government systems but unable to ignore preventable harm.

Do not write Julie as:

- a generic action hero;
- a helpless trauma figure;
- a magic pattern-recognition savant;
- someone who rejects all AI on principle;
- someone who casually volunteers for the system that broke her;
- someone whose core wound resolves quickly.

## Locked Backstory

- The prologue-era incident occurred six years earlier at a forward operating base near the Pakistan border.
- The older system was Argus Beta.
- Julie identified a SIGINT/timestamp pattern that was too perfect and likely engineered.
- Command pressure overrode her warning.
- The later oversight/hearing process reframed her warning as officer hesitation rather than system vulnerability.
- This did not merely embarrass Julie; it taught her that institutions prefer clean certainty to disputed truth.

## Book 1 Technology Canon

- Contractor: Apex Defense.
- Platform family: ARGUS / Argus.
- Prologue variant: Argus Beta.
- Present-day enterprise variant: ARGUS-ENT-4.6.
- Warning-classification module: RoboWarning.
- ARGUS is not sentient.
- Book 1's antagonist path is human exploitation of trusted data/provenance channels.

Naming rule: Treat ARGUS as the platform family, Argus Beta as the older field-grade/FOB variant, ARGUS-ENT-4.6 as the present enterprise system, and RoboWarning as the warning-classification module inside ARGUS-ENT-4.6 for Book 1.

## Book 1 Mechanism Locks

Preserve these elements unless an explicit canon repair changes them:

- continuity premium;
- vendor-enriched provenance;
- normalization hiding self-corroboration;
- whitelisted integration endpoint;
- vendor relay/log purge;
- confidence collapse after contaminated packets are isolated.

The Book 1 crisis should be resolved by restoring uncertainty and pausing action, not by proving the world is safe.

## Recurring Book Engine

Each book should reveal both:

1. the technical or procedural manipulation; and
2. the human motive, incentive, or institutional self-protection behind it.

Avoid "the AI became evil" as an answer. The series should be about human accountability hidden inside automated authority.

## Provisional Canon

These items are useful but may be refined:

- Julie's present-day home base is Culpeper, Virginia.
- Julie's current mask includes farmhouse property, fence work, horses, and deliberate distance from government systems.
- Mara Keene pulls Julie back under supervised conditions.
- Evan Rusk connects the old Argus Beta failure to the present mechanism.
- Vance is a political containment figure tied to oversight optics, appropriations pressure, and ARGUS reputation management.
- Dr. Selene Cho is Apex Defense's technical/executive face.
- Reed Calder is the primary human exploiter/data broker with an old Argus Beta field-trial connection and present-day vendor-provenance role.
- Hargrove returns in Act III as a complicated witness.

## Unresolved Items

Track and resolve carefully:

- Book 1 final title.
- Vance's exact government title.
- Mara Keene's exact agency placement and prior relationship details with Julie.
- Evan Rusk's exact prologue title.
- Dr. Selene Cho's exact Apex title.
- Reed Calder's exact old contractor/field-trial title.
- Exact present-day agency chain.

## Banned Moves

Do not:

- make ARGUS sentient;
- make the exploit a cartoon hack;
- make villains explain everything in one monologue;
- turn technical clues into obvious smoking guns too early;
- erase institutional consequences between books;
- resolve Julie's military trauma as a single-chapter catharsis;
- change locked names, roles, or timeline facts without a canon update;
- add global canon for trivia such as clothing, incidental objects, or one-scene locations.

## Canon Promotion Criteria

A new fact belongs here only if it affects future chapters or books through:

- character continuity;
- recurring relationships;
- backstory;
- technical rules;
- institutional rules;
- timeline locks;
- unresolved series threads;
- book-level mystery logic with future consequences;
- reader-facing series promise.

If a detail is only scene trivia, place it in the book-level `chapter-facts.md` or `continuity-log.md` instead.
