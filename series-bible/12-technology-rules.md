# Technology Rules

## Purpose

Define technology realism rules for the Julie O'Donnell series.

## Core Rule

Systems can be powerful, opaque, and dangerous, but they must remain grounded in plausible infrastructure, data, access, incentives, and human governance.

## AI System Rules

- AI systems produce outputs based on inputs, model design, objective functions, thresholds, training history, and deployment context.
- Confidence is not truth.
- Independent corroboration matters.
- Provenance can be manipulated.
- Integration points create attack surfaces.
- Audit logs can be incomplete, delayed, privileged, or purged.
- Human reviewers may rubber-stamp machine outputs.

## Cyber/Data Rules

- Access should require a plausible path.
- Logs should exist unless there is a reason they do not.
- A whitelisted endpoint is more plausible than a miraculous hack.
- Data manipulation can be quieter than system takeover.
- The most dangerous exploit may look like normal operations.

## ARGUS Naming Rules

- ARGUS: platform family.
- Argus Beta: older prologue-era field/FOB variant.
- ARGUS-ENT-4.6: Book 1 present-day enterprise variant.
- RoboWarning: warning-classification module inside ARGUS-ENT-4.6.

## Explanation Rule

Explain technology through effect, pressure, and investigation first. Only provide deeper mechanism once the story has earned it.

## Banned Technology Moves

- Sentient ARGUS.
- Universal master password.
- Instant impossible decryption.
- Perfect predictive certainty.
- Omniscient surveillance with no infrastructure.
- Dashboard labels that reveal the whole plot.
- Jargon without causality.

## Future Book Requirement

Each book must document its primary system, threat mode, data path, human access path, institutional incentive, and technical reveal ladder in that book's `ai-threat-design.md`.
