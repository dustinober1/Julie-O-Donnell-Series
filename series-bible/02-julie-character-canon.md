# Julie O'Donnell Character Canon

## Core Identity

Julie O'Donnell is a former U.S. Army Captain and Army intelligence officer. She is intelligent, tactical, morally stubborn, and emotionally guarded.

## Defining Wound

Six years earlier, Julie recognized engineered certainty in an AI-enabled SIGINT/targeting feed. Command pressure overrode her warning. The later official record reframed her as hesitant instead of right.

Her wound is not simply guilt. It is the knowledge that institutions often protect clean certainty over disputed truth.

## Present-Day Mask

Julie tries to keep distance from government systems. Her provisional present-day life includes a Culpeper, Virginia farmhouse, horses, fence work, and practical labor that lets her avoid institutional rooms.

## Strengths

- Pattern recognition.
- SIGINT and data skepticism.
- Operational literacy.
- Moral courage under command pressure.
- Ability to see how people hide inside systems.
- Tactical patience when evidence matters.

## Flaws

- Institutional distrust.
- Isolation.
- Anger at bureaucratic self-protection.
- Difficulty trusting old colleagues.
- Tendency to assume official process is already contaminated.
- Trauma response when command pressure repeats.

## Character Rules

Julie should be competent before she is wounded. Her trauma should complicate her judgment, not replace her skill.

She is skeptical of algorithmic confidence, not anti-technology. She understands that systems can be useful and still dangerous when incentives are corrupt.

## Long Arc

Julie should move from private certainty and exile toward public record, shared proof, and institutional confrontation. Do not resolve this too early.

## Forbidden Character Drift

Do not make Julie:

- casually eager to rejoin government work;
- emotionally healed after one confrontation;
- a perfect action superhero;
- a generic anti-AI crusader;
- always right without needing proof;
- cruel or dismissive toward victims whose experience does not match her theory.
