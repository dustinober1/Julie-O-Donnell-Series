# AI Threat Taxonomy

## Purpose

Prevent the series from repeating the same AI threat under new names. Each book should have a distinct system domain, failure mode, human incentive, and reveal pattern.

## Series Principle

The AI is not magic. The danger comes from automation authority plus bad incentives, hidden access, weak governance, contractor pressure, data contamination, institutional self-protection, and human exploitation.

## Threat Categories

### Data Poisoning

Bad data is inserted or manipulated so the model learns or infers the wrong pattern.

### Provenance Laundering

A compromised source appears independent, trusted, or corroborated because metadata and integration paths hide its origin.

### Feedback-Loop Escalation

System outputs affect the real world, and the real-world response becomes new evidence confirming the system.

### Proxy Discrimination

A model avoids direct protected categories but uses proxies that produce harmful classification.

### Confidence Inflation

The system converts weak or dependent signals into high certainty through weighting, normalization, or repeated echo sources.

### Automation Bias

Humans defer to outputs because the system is institutionalized, expensive, or politically protected.

### Hidden Objective Mismatch

The deployed system optimizes for a measurable target that conflicts with public safety or justice.

### Audit Suppression

Logs, traceability, or appeal pathways are incomplete, purged, delayed, or buried under privilege claims.

### Synthetic Evidence Injection

Generated, altered, or context-stripped evidence enters trusted pipelines and creates false reality.

### Human-in-the-Loop Corruption

The human reviewer exists formally but functionally rubber-stamps machine output.

## Book 1 Threat Lock

Book 1 uses provenance laundering and confidence inflation through:

- continuity premium;
- vendor-enriched provenance;
- normalization hiding self-corroboration;
- whitelisted integration endpoint;
- vendor relay/log purge;
- confidence collapse after contaminated packets are isolated.

## Future Book Variation Targets

Possible later books:

- medical triage/risk model: proxy variables and hidden objective mismatch;
- predictive policing: feedback-loop escalation and automation bias;
- financial fraud/credit invisibility: audit suppression and proxy exclusion;
- child welfare risk scoring: high-stakes classification and appeal failure;
- autonomous procurement: evaluation gaming and contractor capture;
- civic influence/election trust: synthetic evidence and narrative amplification;
- final ARGUS reckoning: governance failure and institutional memory suppression.

## Reuse Rule

Do not reuse Book 1's exact provenance/self-corroboration mechanism as the main engine of a later book unless the repetition is part of the long-series ARGUS reckoning.
