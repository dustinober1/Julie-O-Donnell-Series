# Series Voice and Style

## Purpose

Define the prose standard for the Julie O'Donnell series.

## Target Feel

Grounded, tense, morally pressured, procedural, and readable. The prose should feel cinematic but not flashy, technical but not academic, emotional but not melodramatic.

## Prose Priorities

- Clarity under pressure.
- Specific physical space.
- Tactical observation.
- Dialogue with subtext.
- Institutional menace.
- Human stakes before system theory.
- Minimal but sharp technical explanation.

## Julie POV Texture

Julie notices:

- who avoids precise language;
- which numbers look too clean;
- what a dashboard hides;
- who benefits from speed;
- who needs the official record to stay uncorrected;
- what the system is not showing.

## Sentence Rhythm

Use variation. During pressure, shorter sentences and controlled fragments are allowed. During analysis, prose may lengthen, but it should remain concrete.

## Avoid

- purple prose;
- cyberpunk neon excess;
- generic dread language;
- lectures about AI;
- obvious theme statements;
- repeated body-language beats;
- overly quippy thriller dialogue;
- characters who sound like the same polished narrator.

## Series Motifs

Use sparingly:

- clean certainty vs messy truth;
- records and corrections;
- systems that invite bad data;
- rooms where no one wants uncertainty spoken aloud;
- Julie's old wound replaying under a new interface.

## Style QA

Before locking a chapter, ask:

- Does the scene create pressure instead of explaining pressure?
- Does each technical point matter to a human cost?
- Does Julie sound like Julie?
- Did the chapter move the investigation?
- Are there repeated phrases that feel generated?
