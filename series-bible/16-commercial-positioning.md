# Commercial Positioning

## Purpose

Keep the Julie O'Donnell series aligned with reader-facing thriller expectations while preserving the unique AI/institutional angle.

## Genre Lane

Primary: contemporary intelligence thriller.

Secondary lanes:

- military techno-thriller;
- political thriller;
- AI governance / algorithmic escalation thriller;
- investigative thriller.

## Reader Appeal

Readers should come for:

- high-stakes intelligence pressure;
- a damaged but competent protagonist;
- plausible AI/system threats;
- government and contractor intrigue;
- moral stakes;
- smart clue logic;
- escalating institutional danger.

## Positioning Line

A former Army intelligence officer who once saw an AI targeting system lie is pulled into new crises where machine certainty hides human manipulation.

## What the Series Is Not

- Not cyberpunk.
- Not space opera.
- Not an evil-AI apocalypse.
- Not a lecture about technology.
- Not a pure military action series.
- Not a cozy procedural.

## Comparative Energy

Use as directional energy, not copycat template:

- Jack Ryan: geopolitical escalation and institutional stakes.
- Jason Bourne: damaged competent outsider energy.
- Homeland: intelligence pressure and distrust.
- Near-future AI thriller: algorithmic harm and system opacity.

## Marketing Hooks

- What if the system was wrong — and everyone in power needed it to be right?
- The machine did not lie. Someone taught it what to trust.
- Certainty is the most dangerous weapon in the room.
- She warned them once. They buried the record. Now the system is back.

## Cover/Blurb Promise

Covers and blurbs should communicate:

- modern intelligence thriller;
- AI/system threat;
- institutional conspiracy/containment;
- high stakes;
- grounded realism;
- Julie as central human anchor.
