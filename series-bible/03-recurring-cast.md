# Recurring Cast

## Purpose

Track recurring characters, relationships, roles, unresolved questions, and continuity rules across the Julie O'Donnell series.

## Julie O'Donnell

- Former U.S. Army Captain / Army intelligence officer.
- Protagonist.
- Defined by Argus Beta, institutional betrayal, and unwilling return to automated decision crises.

## Mara Keene

- Provisional role: old colleague and current government access controller.
- Function: pulls Julie back under supervised conditions.
- Relationship pressure: wants to believe Julie but must manage institutional constraints.
- Open questions: exact agency placement, exact prior relationship with Julie, current authority level.

## Evan Rusk

- Provisional role: former prologue-era lead analyst/internal skeptic.
- Function: connects old Argus Beta failure to present mechanism.
- Relationship pressure: may carry guilt, doubt, or compromised memory of the original incident.
- Open questions: exact prologue title, why he did not fully back Julie then, what changed now.

## Vance

- Provisional role: political containment figure.
- Function: protects oversight optics, appropriations pressure, and ARGUS reputation management.
- Open questions: exact title, personal stake, relationship to Apex Defense and committees.

## Dr. Selene Cho

- Provisional role: Apex Defense executive/technical face.
- Function: defends ARGUS while privately recognizing the vulnerability may be real.
- Open questions: exact title, ethical line, complicity level, whether she recurs after Book 1.

## Reed Calder

- Provisional role: primary human exploiter/data broker.
- Function: connects old Argus Beta field-trial path to present vendor-provenance abuse.
- Open questions: exact old contractor title, current vendor role, personal motive.

## Hargrove

- Prologue command-pressure figure.
- Function: represents command culture that preferred action through automated certainty.
- Book 1 role: returns in Act III as complicated witness.
- Open questions: rank/title at present, responsibility level, whether he is cowardly, complicit, or trapped.

## Cast Rules

- Recurring characters should carry consequences between books.
- No one should reset to a generic ally or antagonist after a major case.
- Institutional roles must be specific enough to create pressure but not so detailed they break plausibility.
- Relationship evolution should be slow, earned, and tied to proof, trust, and risk.
