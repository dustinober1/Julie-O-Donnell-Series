# Series Premise and Promise

## Series Premise

Julie O'Donnell once saw an AI-enabled military intelligence system lie with perfect confidence. Her warning was overridden, the record was cleaned, and her career was reframed as hesitation instead of truth.

Years later, as newer versions of automated warning, risk, and decision systems spread through government, defense, law enforcement, finance, medicine, and civic life, Julie is repeatedly pulled into crises where machine certainty hides human manipulation.

## Commercial Hook

What if the system was wrong — and everyone in power needed it to be right?

## Reader Promise

Each installment should deliver:

- a high-concept AI or algorithmic threat;
- grounded investigative momentum;
- escalating institutional pressure;
- a human victim or moral cost;
- a plausible technical mechanism;
- a villain or exploiter with human motives;
- Julie forced to confront the old wound in a new form.

## Tone Promise

The series should feel tense, procedural, intelligent, morally pressured, and contemporary. It should not feel like cyberpunk, military fantasy, or abstract AI discourse.

## Series Question

Can Julie force powerful institutions to preserve uncertainty when certainty is what gives them permission to act?

## Book-Level Variation

Each book should use a different system domain and failure mode so the series stays fresh.

Examples:

- national-security warning classification;
- hospital triage or medical risk scoring;
- predictive policing;
- financial fraud and credit invisibility;
- child welfare or family risk algorithms;
- autonomous systems procurement;
- civic trust and election influence;
- final ARGUS/AI-governance reckoning.
