# Mystery and Clue Rules

## Purpose

Maintain fair, escalating clue logic across Julie O'Donnell books.

## Mystery Principle

The reader should eventually understand how Julie could have solved the case earlier, but should not feel the final answer was obvious from the start.

## Clue Types

- Data anomaly.
- Missing provenance.
- Overconfident classification.
- Dependent source disguised as independent source.
- Witness contradiction.
- Institutional overreaction.
- Log gap.
- Vendor access clue.
- Timeline inconsistency.
- Human motive clue.
- Official record language clue.

## Clue Ladder

A book should generally move through:

1. strange output;
2. human cost;
3. official explanation;
4. contradiction;
5. false explanation;
6. technical narrowing;
7. access/motive link;
8. proof under pressure;
9. public or official record confrontation.

## Red Herring Rules

A red herring should:

- be plausible;
- teach the reader something true;
- reveal institutional pressure or human motive;
- not exist only to waste time;
- not contradict the final mechanism.

## Fair Play Rule

Do not hide the final answer entirely off-page. The reader must have access to enough clues to understand the solution once revealed.

## Julie-Specific Rule

Julie should notice patterns, but she must still need proof, allies, documents, logs, witnesses, and institutional leverage.

## Book 1 Clue Guardrail

Book 1 clues must support provenance laundering and confidence inflation, not a generic hack or sentient-system twist.
