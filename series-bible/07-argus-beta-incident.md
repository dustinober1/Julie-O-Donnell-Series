# Argus Beta Incident

## Purpose

Preserve the original wound event that drives Julie O'Donnell's long-series arc.

## Locked Setup

- Time: six years before Book 1 present day.
- Location: forward operating base near the Pakistan border.
- Julie's role: U.S. Army Captain / intelligence officer.
- System: Argus Beta.
- Conflict: Julie identifies a SIGINT/timestamp pattern that looks too perfect and likely engineered.
- Pressure: command wants action and treats system confidence as operational permission.
- Outcome: Julie's warning is overridden and later reframed.

## Core Emotional Truth

Julie was not punished because she failed to see the system. She was punished because she saw the system too clearly when the institution needed certainty.

## Official Record Problem

The later hearing/oversight record reframed Julie's warning as hesitation. This is a central wound. Future books should not erase the damage of the official record.

## Technical Shape

Argus Beta should feel like an earlier, field-grade or FOB-era system in the ARGUS family. It should not be as mature or enterprise-integrated as ARGUS-ENT-4.6.

## What Julie Recognized

- The pattern was too neat.
- The timestamps/signals aligned too perfectly.
- Confidence appeared engineered rather than earned.
- The system was swallowing garbage as corroboration.

## What Not to Do

- Do not make Argus Beta sentient.
- Do not reveal every detail of the old incident too early.
- Do not make Julie simply traumatized; she had valid analytical reasons.
- Do not make Hargrove a cartoon. He should represent command pressure and institutional incentives.

## Long-Series Function

The Argus Beta incident should echo across the series through:

- Julie's distrust of clean confidence;
- official records that erase uncertainty;
- old colleagues who remember differently;
- contractor and command cultures that continue under new names;
- recurring ARGUS-family consequences.
