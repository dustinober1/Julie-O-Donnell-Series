# Continuity Locks

## Purpose

Record non-negotiable continuity rules for the Julie O'Donnell series.

## Global Locks

- Julie O'Donnell is the protagonist.
- Julie is a former U.S. Army Captain and Army intelligence officer.
- The Argus Beta incident occurred six years before Book 1 present day.
- The prologue-era setting is a forward operating base near the Pakistan border.
- Julie saw engineered certainty in a SIGINT/timestamp pattern.
- Command pressure overrode her warning.
- The official record later reframed her warning as hesitation.
- Julie's wound is tied to institutional preference for clean certainty over disputed truth.

## Technology Locks

- ARGUS is a platform family, not a sentient antagonist.
- Argus Beta is the older prologue-era variant.
- ARGUS-ENT-4.6 is the Book 1 present enterprise variant.
- RoboWarning is a warning-classification module inside ARGUS-ENT-4.6.
- Apex Defense is the Book 1 contractor tied to ARGUS.

## Book 1 Mechanism Locks

- continuity premium;
- vendor-enriched provenance;
- normalization hiding self-corroboration;
- whitelisted integration endpoint;
- vendor relay/log purge;
- confidence collapse after contaminated packets are isolated.

## Character Locks

- Julie is competent, not merely traumatized.
- Julie is skeptical of algorithmic confidence, not reflexively anti-technology.
- Julie should be pressured back in, not casually volunteer.
- Mara Keene is the old colleague/access controller in Book 1.
- Hargrove is the prologue command-pressure figure.

## Resolution Locks

- Book 1 resolves the immediate crisis by restoring uncertainty and pausing action.
- The ending should not prove that the system is safe.
- The ending should not erase the institutional cover story entirely.
- The long-series ARGUS reckoning should remain available after Book 1.

## Change Rule

Any change to this file requires a canon update report and an entry in `series-bible/canon-change-log.md`.
