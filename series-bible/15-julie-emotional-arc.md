# Julie Emotional Arc

## Purpose

Track Julie O'Donnell's emotional progression across the series so she develops without resolving too quickly.

## Starting State

Julie begins the series in practical exile from the systems that broke her. She is competent and functional, but guarded. She distrusts institutional certainty because she has seen how it can erase truth.

## Core Wound

Julie was right under pressure, but the institution needed her to be wrong. The wound is not only trauma from failure; it is trauma from the official record replacing reality.

## Book 1 Emotional Movement

Book 1 should move Julie through:

1. avoidance;
2. reluctant reentry;
3. recognition of the old pattern in new form;
4. pressure from people who once knew her;
5. need for proof instead of private certainty;
6. confrontation with the official record;
7. partial restoration through corrected uncertainty, not full vindication.

## Series-Level Movement

Across the series, Julie should move from private recognition to public record, from isolation to selective trust, and from reacting to Argus-shaped failures to actively forcing accountability.

## Emotional Guardrails

- Do not heal Julie in one book.
- Do not keep her static for eight books.
- Do not make every case simply trigger the same memory.
- Let each case test a different aspect of her wound.
- Let trust be earned through action, risk, and proof.

## Emotional Stakes by Relationship

- Mara: trust under institutional constraint.
- Evan: memory, guilt, and old analytical failure.
- Hargrove: command pressure and official record.
- Cho: expert recognition and corporate silence.
- Calder: exploitation of what Julie once warned about.

## Completion Condition

The long arc should not end with Julie proving every system is corrupt. It should end with her forcing a truth-preserving process that institutions cannot easily erase.
